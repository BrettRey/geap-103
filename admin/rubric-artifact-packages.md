# GEAP 103 -- Artifact Packages Rubric (25%)

**Course:** GEAP 103 Basic Computer Skills
**Assessment:** Artifact Packages
**Weight:** 25% of final grade
**Primary CLOs:** 1 (Manage files and versions), 4 (Evaluate AI outputs), 6 (Produce digital products to college standards)

---

## Overview

Three artifact packages of increasing complexity. Each package includes the product itself AND the documentation, explanation, and sharing that surround it. The artifact is the communicative package, not just the thing.

| Package | Week | Weight | Requirements |
|---------|------|--------|--------------|
| Organised file system | 4 | 7% | Logical folder structure, consistent naming, version awareness |
| Mid-semester artifact | 10 | 8% | A working artifact addressing a genuine need, with accessibility where applicable, accompanied by process documentation and evidence of sharing |
| Final artifact | 14 | 10% | More complex or ambitious artifact, iteration visible, documentation shows how the work was explained to others |

*The artifact types are not constrained to Office 365 outputs. Students may build documents, spreadsheets, presentations, visualisations, apps, interactive tools, data pipelines, or whatever serves their goal. The assessment is on the quality of the communicative package: can the student articulate what they built, why, how, and what they learned?*

---

## Package Components

Each artifact package includes:

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Product | A digital artifact: document, tool, visualisation, application, or whatever the student built |
| Process documentation | In the student's own words: what AI did, what the student changed and why, what was checked |
| Verification note | How the student verified quality (ran Accessibility Checker, tested on sample, checked formatting, etc.) |
| Evidence of sharing | How the student presented, described, or taught the work to someone else |

---

## Grading Criteria

| Level | Description | % Range |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Excellent | Ambitious attempt; product is functional and accessible; process documentation shows thoughtful AI collaboration in student's own language; evidence of sharing/explaining the work to others; student can articulate what they built, why, and what they learned | 85--100 |
| Proficient | Substantive attempt; product is functional and mostly accessible; process documentation present and in own words; some evidence of sharing | 70--84 |
| Developing | Safe or trivial attempt; product functional with issues; process documentation incomplete or possibly AI-generated; limited evidence of sharing | 50--69 |
| Insufficient | Minimal attempt; non-functional product; missing process documentation | 0--49 |

*Note on ambition: "Ambitious" and "substantive" are calibrated to B1. A student who attempts something that stretches their ability to describe it is being ambitious. A student who sticks to what they can already articulate easily is playing it safe. The framework values the stretch.*

## Per-Package Specific Requirements

### Package 1: Organised File System (Week 4, 7%)

- **Product:** geap-103 folder in OneDrive with logical subfolder structure (at minimum: week-01, week-02, week-03), consistent naming (lowercase-with-hyphens recommended), files in the right places
- **Process documentation:** 2--3 sentences describing how the student organised their files, what naming convention they chose and why, and what they would change
- **Verification:** Files are in the right folders, naming is consistent, Goal Document exists with content
- **Evidence of sharing:** Submitted via OneDrive share link; peer response activity in Week 4 provides sharing context
- **Late submission:** Students may submit by end of Week 5 with no penalty

| What to check | Evidence |
|---------------|----------|
| geap-103 folder exists in OneDrive | Folder visible |
| Subfolders exist | At least week-01 through week-03 |
| Files are in the right places | Files from each week in the corresponding folder |
| Goal Document exists with content | Entries from Weeks 1--3 present |
| Naming is consistent | One convention applied throughout |

### Package 2: Mid-Semester Artifact (Week 10, 8%)

- **Product:** A working artifact addressing a genuine need, with accessibility features where applicable
- **Process documentation:** Written in the student's own words, covering what AI did, what the student changed and why, what was checked, what was shared with others and how
- **Verification:** Accessibility Checker run where applicable; specific checks documented
- **Evidence of sharing:** At least one documented instance of presenting, describing, or teaching the work to someone else (e.g., peer review, in-class explanation, session review transcript)
- **Feedback:** Written feedback returned by Week 12, with attention to whether the student's ambition has grown and whether the communicative package is developing

### Package 3: Final Artifact (Week 14, 10%)

- **Product:** More complex or ambitious artifact than Package 2; iteration visible (version history, commit log, or documented revisions)
- **Process documentation:** Written in the student's own words, showing the full collaboration arc: what AI did, what the student directed, what the student changed and why, what the student checked or verified, what was shared with others and how
- **Verification:** Accessibility features, quality checks documented
- **Evidence of sharing:** Documentation of how the work was explained to others; presented during the Week 14 sharing/gallery session (1-minute explanation of one design decision)
- **Assessment:** Summative (no further feedback cycle)

## Process Documentation Authenticity Check

Compare the language in process documentation against the student's demonstrated oral level at micro-defences. A marked mismatch (e.g., fluent academic prose from a student who struggles to sustain oral turns) warrants a brief oral follow-up: "Can you tell me in your own words what the AI did here?"

Process documentation is required for each package, written in the student's own words (not AI-generated):

- What AI did and what the student directed
- What the student changed and why
- What the student checked or verified
- What was shared with others and how (peer review, presentation, explanation)

---

## Accessibility Requirements

Where the artifact is a document, presentation, or other content intended for an audience, accessibility features are expected:

- Headings
- Alt text
- Accessible colour
- Other audience-appropriate considerations

The specific tools used to check accessibility may vary; the principle is that the student considered their audience.

---

## Evidence of Sharing: Minimum Threshold

At least one documented instance of sharing, presenting, describing, or teaching the work to someone else. Concrete examples that count:

- Explained the artifact to a peer during a micro-defence triad
- Shared the artifact via OneDrive and received written feedback from a partner
- Described the artifact to AI during a session review (transcript saved)
- Presented a design decision during the Week 14 gallery session
- Wrote a collaboration note or handoff documentation for a group member
- Demonstrated the artifact to the instructor during circulation

"Evidence of sharing" means the student can show that they communicated about the work to another person (or AI in a structured practice), not just that they built it privately.
