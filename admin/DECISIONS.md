# GEAP 103 Decisions Log

## 2026-03-25

- **Triad defences for all micro-defence rounds (Weeks 4, 8, 11).** Individual defences created overflow and idle students. Triads get all students through in one 30-min block. Instructor samples ~15/21 presenters per round with observation tracking to ensure equitable coverage across rounds.

- **Assessment timeline staggered (Mollick proposal).** MD3→Wk 11, DLs→Wk 11, Git Episodes→Wk 12, Oral Reflection→Wk 13, Final Artifact→Wk 14. No week exceeds ~38%, and the heavy week (11) pairs curation with a triad block that doesn't compete cognitively.

- **Evolving criteria 3→5→7.** Week 4 diagnostic baseline uses 3 criteria. Week 8 adds CLO 2 (troubleshooting). Week 11 adds CLOs 3–4 (AI direction/evaluation). Equal weighting across rounds (~8.3% each). First three criteria constant for longitudinal comparability.

- **Oral reflection replaces written.** One-on-one, 4 min, unmediated, with pre-tagging (5 min whole-class setup). Stronger validity at B1 than written. Week 13, other students finalize artifacts. Classes above 20 may need adjusted timing.

- **Weekly speaking practice formalized.** Session review becomes a specific scenario each week, matched to the week's learning. Students say the role-setting prompt aloud in voice mode. The prompt deliberately withholds vocabulary modelling to force production.

- **Faculty prep module is a self-contained AI prompt.** Delivered as .html via GitHub Pages. Text conversation (voice mode starts a new chat, can't carry the 1,800-word setup prompt). Five challenges with teacher flips.

- **GEAP 103 is a new course, not a replacement for EAPC 106.** The design notes already said this; the README was wrong. Fixed.

- **Pandoc conversion deferred to Phase 4.** .md is the source format; .docx/.html for distribution happens once at content lock.

- **GitHub repo published.** BrettRey/geap-103, CC BY 4.0, GitHub Pages for .html files. Email drafts gitignored.

## 2026-03-27/28

- **AWL vocabulary over task-specific terms.** From Week 6 onward, formal vocabulary is AWL words (data, formula, interpret, verify, chart; collaborate, contribute, specific, context, respond; etc.). Task-specific terms (cell, row, pull request, fork) introduced by pointing during worked examples.

- **Sentence frames optional from Week 5.** Labelled "optional — use if you're stuck." Prevents frames from becoming a ceiling for stronger students.

- **Structured A/B turn-taking in all pair work.** "Partner A speaks first, then Partner B." Prevents the confident partner from dominating.

- **Session review self-check added.** After each session review: "Write one/two sentences: what did AI ask you to clarify?" Zero-cost diagnostic of whether the interaction worked.

- **GitHub accounts as hard prerequisite before Week 3.** Assigned as homework in Week 2 closing. Account creation in class consumes 15-20 min of unpredictable friction.

- **Git maintenance in Week 5.** 10-min structured "commit before fixing, commit after fixing, check timeline." Bridges the Weeks 3-6 practice gap flagged by Guzdial.

- **Thread map visual at Week 4.** Shows GD/DL/Git portfolio purposes and locations together for the first time. Addresses Mayer's three-thread cumulative load concern.

- **DL feedback Round 2 moved to Week 11 opening (digital distribution before class).** Students have zero bandwidth at end of Week 10 (artifact submission day). Mayer and Chen flagged this.

- **DL deadline decoupled from micro-defence.** 11:59 PM, not end of class. Prevents DL anxiety from contaminating defence performance. Kunnan flagged fairness concern.

- **Micro-Defence 3 confirmed in Week 11, not 12.** Old lesson plan had it in Week 12. AF v6 clearly says Week 11. Fixed.

- **Oral reflection is oral only.** AF v6 specifies a 4-min conversation, not a written document. Old plans had students writing a 600-800 word reflection. Corrected.

- **Gallery walk default for classes of 15+.** Round-robin (30 sec/student × 20) takes too long. Gallery walk for large classes, round-robin for small.

- **Scoring anchors for all 7 micro-defence criteria.** Criteria 1-5 anchors added to match the existing 6-7 anchors. Guzdial flagged the inconsistency.

- **Justified acceptance valued in DL rubric.** Well-reasoned acceptance of good AI output is valid evidence, not just modification/rejection. Mollick flagged this as models improve.

- **Git Episodes collaboration "or equivalent" operationalised.** Three concrete alternatives: tracked changes + handoff note, OneDrive version history, GitHub Issue thread. Guzdial flagged the vague parenthetical.

- **Firm time limits on Week 14 presentations.** 2 min individual (hard stop), 3 min group. Simultaneous sharing for classes over 18. Chen flagged the pacing collapse risk.

- **Transfer table as pair task, not instructor monologue.** Students match skills to future contexts, then instructor reveals. Ellis flagged passive reception.

- **Whole-sequence review (Weeks 1-7) conducted.** 5 R&R, 1 Accept. Cross-cutting fixes adopted (above). Set aside: retrofit info-gap tasks into Weeks 1-2, DL peer review, collapse three threads, replace Git with shared docs, make GD graded.

- **Sample project ideas sourced from Mollick/Cummins/Willison.** 21 curated ideas in 4 tiers (identity+surprise, practical+personal, creative+ambitious, professional). Cummins' identity texts framework and Willison's "build it and ship it" ethos applied.
