# GEAP 103 Week 9: Instructor Materials Package

**Title:** Building My Thing (Part 1)
**Phase:** Building (Week 9 of 14)
**Contact time:** 3 x 55 min (165 min total: 150 min instruction + 15 min break)
**Date prepared:** 2026-03-28

This file contains everything you need to prepare slides and run the class for Week 9. The lesson plan (`lesson-plans/week-09.md`) has the pedagogical rationale. This file has the concrete content. In case of conflict between this materials package and the lesson plan for Week 9, this materials package governs (it reflects March 2026 design revisions). For anything not covered here, the lesson plan governs.

**Week 9 design note:** Students start building. They have spent 8 weeks developing the language to describe what they want. Now they use it to direct their own work. The session structure: self-selected demonstrations by project type, then sustained project work, then a peer check-in that evaluates alignment between stated intention and artifact-in-progress. No graded submission this week; the artifact package is due Week 10. The Goal Document entry this week is the task list itself.

**New vocabulary (5 AWL terms):** design, draft, evaluate, requirement, focus

*Presentation-specific terms (slide, layout, speaker notes, caption, colour contrast) are introduced contextually during the presentation group demonstration. They are genre-specific terms, not AWL vocabulary.*

**AI tools:** Same tool-agnostic approach. Students use whatever AI they can access during project work. The instructor redirects students to AI first when they get stuck: "Have you asked Copilot? What did it say?" AI role during session review: debugging partner.

## Segment A: Vocabulary + Self-Selected Demonstrations (0:00–0:40)

### Vocabulary Pre-Training (0:00–0:12)

**Vocabulary reference slide** (display and leave up):

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **design** | The intentional plan for how your artifact will work and look |
| **draft** | A preliminary version -- meant to be changed |
| **evaluate** | To judge the quality of something against criteria |
| **requirement** | Something the project must do or include |
| **focus** | To concentrate attention on one specific thing |

**Teach these connected to today's work:**

1. **Design:**

   > "The intentional plan for how your artifact will work and look. Everyone is designing something this semester. 'I designed it this way because...' is the language of explaining your choices."

2. **Draft:**

   > "A preliminary version. What you build today is a first draft. A draft is meant to be changed. 'This is my first draft' is not an apology -- it's a normal step in the process."

3. **Evaluate:**

   > "To judge the quality of something against criteria. When your partner looks at your work and asks 'does this match your plan?', they are evaluating. You've been evaluating AI output since Week 2. Now you evaluate your own work."

4. **Requirement:**

   > "Something the project must do or include. 'My requirement is that a screen reader can navigate it.' Requirements come from your Goal Document -- they are what you said you wanted."

5. **Focus:**

   > "To concentrate attention on one specific thing. 'Today I will focus on the layout' means you're not doing everything -- you're choosing one task. Focus is how you turn a big plan into a work session."

**Pair task** (3 min): Display:

> **Vague:** "I'm going to work on my project today."
>
> Rewrite this using at least 3 of the 5 vocabulary terms. Make it specific.

Share 1-2 examples. Quick check: "Which terms did you use? Is the new version specific enough to work from?"

### Self-Selected Project-Type Demonstrations (0:12–0:35)

> "You've been refining your ideas for 8 weeks. Today you start building. First, a short demonstration relevant to what you're actually making."

Announce groupings. Students move to sit with their project-type group.

**Group options** (combine small groups as needed):

| Group | What they're building | Demonstration focus |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Document / novel** | Structured or narrative content | Turning a rough outline into a structured, accessible document with Copilot + Accessibility Checker |
| **Data tool** | Excel-based projects | Multi-sheet workbooks, linking summary data across sheets, creating a chart |
| **Presentation / video** | Slides or Clipchamp | Generating slides from an outline, writing speaker notes, adding captions. *Introduce slide, layout, speaker notes, caption, colour contrast here.* |
| **Game / app** | Interactive browser content | Describing a simple quiz to AI and testing the output |
| **Art** | AI image generation + design layout | Generating images with a specific prompt and arranging them |
| **Combination** | Multi-tool projects | Embedding a chart in a report, linking outputs across tools |

**Logistics for one instructor:** Run the 2 largest groups as sequential live demos (~10 min each). Other groups follow prepared step-by-step guides at their own pace (via OneDrive). Instructor floats to guide groups between live demos.

**At least one live demo should model AI failure:** e.g., AI applies heading styles incorrectly or generates an inaccessible layout. Diagnose and revise on screen. This prepares students for the gaps they'll encounter during building.

**What to say while circulating among self-paced groups:**

| What you see | What to say |
|-------------|------------|
| Students stuck on a step in the guide | "What step are you on? Read me what it says. What part is unclear?" |
| Students finished the guide early | "Write your gallery walk summary now -- what's the most useful thing you learned?" |
| Students want to watch a different group's demo | "Stay with your group for the guide. You'll see what other groups learned in the gallery walk." |

### Gallery Walk (0:35–0:40)

Each group writes a 1-sentence summary on a shared slide or sticky note:

> "The most useful thing we learned is _____."

Students circulate and read all groups' summaries (3-5 min). This gives everyone exposure to techniques relevant to other project types.

## Segment B: Planning + Project Work (0:40–1:35)

### Pre-Task Planning (0:40–0:50)

> "Open your Goal Document. Re-read what you wrote in Weeks 7 and 8 -- your project plan, your risk assessment. Now write a concrete task list for today. Not 'work on my project.' Specific tasks with specific requirements."

Students write a task list directly in their Goal Document. This IS the Week 9 entry (entry 9).

**Prompt** (display):

> Write your task list for today. What specifically will you build in the next hour? What are the requirements?

**Sentence frames** *(optional -- use if you're stuck)*:

> - "Today I will focus on _____. The requirement is _____."
> - "The first thing I need to do is _____ because _____."
> - "My draft should include _____ by the end of today."
> - "If I finish _____, I will move on to _____."

**After writing** (5 min): Structured A/B turn-taking.

> **Partner A** reads their task list aloud (1.5 min). **Partner B** asks one clarifying question. Switch.
>
> "Does your partner's list make sense? Is anything missing?"

---

### Project Work (0:50–1:35)

> "Build. This is not free time. You have a task list. Work through it."

Students build toward the specific tasks they defined.

**Process documentation mini-template** (display or have students add to their Goal Document):

> | AI prompt | AI output | My decision | Why |
> |-----------|-----------|-------------|-----|
> | | | | |
>
> "Every time you ask AI to do something, add a row. 30 seconds per row. This saves you from trying to remember next week."

This documentation becomes part of the artifact package submitted in Week 10.

**What to say while circulating:**

| What you see | What to say |
|-------------|------------|
| Student working without a visible task list | "Show me your task list. Which task are you on?" |
| Student stuck | "Have you asked Copilot? What did it say?" |
| Student accepted AI output without checking | "What did AI do? What did you change? Write that down." |
| Student finished a task | "When you finish this task, write a sentence about what AI did and what you changed." |
| Student not documenting anything | "You'll need process documentation for the artifact package next week. Write a note now while you remember." |
| Student working well, on track | "Good. What's next on your list?" |
| Student has no idea where to start | "Look at your task list. What's the first item? Let's start there." |

**Hard documentation pause at 1:15** (announce to whole class):

> "Stop building for 2 minutes. Open your process table. Write at least 3 rows: what did AI do, what did you decide, why. If you haven't been tracking, reconstruct from memory now. This documentation is required for the artifact package next week."

Then students resume building until 1:35.

**Decision Log entry** (optional this week): Students who want to write a DL entry should document a significant AI interaction during project work. Remind: 8 entries total across Weeks 2-11; this is one of the eligible weeks.

## Segment C: Peer Check-In + Session Review (1:50–2:45)

### Peer Check-In (1:50–2:08)

> "Find your partner. Show each other what you've been building. The question: does this match what you described in your Goal Document?"

**Setup:** Both partners open their Goal Document (Weeks 7-8 entries) alongside their artifact, ideally side by side (split screen or two tabs). This reduces the split-attention cost of switching between documents.

**Structured A/B turn-taking:**

**Partner A goes first** (8 min):

| Step | Time | What happens |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Show** | 3 min | Student A shows their artifact-in-progress |
| **Compare** | 3 min | Partner B opens A's Goal Document (Weeks 7-8 entries). "Does this match the plan? What's different?" |
| **Feedback** | 2 min | Partner B says one thing that matches and one thing that seems missing |

**Partner B goes next** (8 min): Same process, reversed.

**Guidance on screen:**

> When you evaluate your partner's work, ask:
> - Does this look like what they described in their Goal Document?
> - What's here that they planned?
> - What's missing?
> - What's here that they didn't plan?

**What to say while circulating:**

| What you see | What to say |
|-------------|------------|
| Partner giving vague feedback ("looks good") | "Be specific. What matches the Goal Document? What doesn't?" |
| Student can't find partner's Goal Document | Help them locate it. The comparison is the point. |
| Pair finished early | "Write one sentence: how well does your artifact match your plan so far?" |
| Student's artifact is far from the plan | "That's useful information. What changed? Is it a better plan, or did you get off track?" |

---

### Decision Log / Continued Work + Commit (2:08–2:20)

Students who are writing a Decision Log entry this week: complete it now using the standard template.

Students who are not: continue project work or refine process documentation notes.

At 2:18:

> "Commit your work now. Descriptive message: what did you accomplish today? Not 'Updated files.'"

---

### Bridging Activity (2:20–2:22)

> "In two minutes, you'll explain something to AI. Think about this: you built something today, and it probably doesn't perfectly match what you intended. What's the gap? What did you want, and what did you get?"

Two minutes of silence. Students think and prepare.

---

### Session Review -- Speaking Practice (2:22–2:35)

Display:

> **Open your AI tool. Start a new chat.**
>
> **First, say this (or paste it):**
> "Speaking practice. I'm a B1 English student practising explaining my work. Ask me follow-up questions. If my explanation is vague, tell me what's unclear and ask me to be more specific. Don't do the explaining for me."
>
> **Then, your task:** You built something but it doesn't do what you intended. Explain to AI what you wanted, what you got, and what you think went wrong.

AI role: Debugging partner.

**Voice mode encouraged.** Remind students: voice mode starts a new chat. Paste the role-setting prompt first, then switch to speaking.

Students talk to AI for 10-12 minutes, describing the gap between their Goal Document plan and their artifact draft. The AI pushes back when the description is vague: "What specifically doesn't match? What did you expect to see?"

Instructor circulates and listens. No intervention unless a student is stuck or silent.

**What to listen for:**

| What you hear | What it means |
|--------------|---------------|
| Student describes a specific gap between plan and artifact | Good production -- the self-evaluation skill is developing |
| Student says "it doesn't work" and AI pushes back | The system is working -- AI is demanding specificity |
| Student is silent | Approach quietly: "Start with one thing that's different from what you planned." |
| Student and AI are having a genuine back-and-forth about what went wrong | Let it run. This is the goal. |

**Self-check** (1 min):

> "Write two sentences: What was the gap between your plan and your draft? What did AI help you see?"
>
> *If students are visibly exhausted, simplify: "Tell AI one thing you're proud of from today's building."*

---

### Closing (2:35–2:45)

> "You started building. Look at your task list. What did you finish? What didn't you get to? That gap is useful information for next week."
>
> "Next week: you finish and submit your mid-semester artifact package. That means three things: the artifact itself, your process documentation in your own words, and evidence of sharing. The peer check-in today counts as sharing."
>
> "Your artifact package includes three things: what you built, documentation of how you built it, and evidence that you explained it to someone."

Remind:

> "If you haven't committed your work, do it now. Descriptive message."

## Catch-Up Instructions for Latecomers

### If You Missed Week 9

In Week 9, we started building our mid-semester artifacts and practised evaluating whether early work matches our stated intentions.

**1. Learn these 5 terms:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **design** | The intentional plan for how your artifact will work and look |
| **draft** | A preliminary version -- meant to be changed |
| **evaluate** | To judge the quality of something against criteria |
| **requirement** | Something the project must do or include |
| **focus** | To concentrate attention on one specific thing |

**2. Start building your artifact.** Open the tools relevant to your project (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, browser, etc.). Begin working on what you described in your Goal Document Weeks 7-8 entries. Use AI to help, but document what AI does and what you change.

**3. Update your Goal Document.** Write entry 9: a concrete task list for your next work session. Use specific language:
- "Today I will focus on _____. The requirement is _____."
- "The first thing I need to do is _____ because _____."
- "My draft should include _____ by the end of today."

**4. Document your process.** As you build, write brief notes about what AI did, what you changed, and what you checked. You need this for the artifact package (due Week 10).

**5. Self-evaluate alignment.** Compare what you've built so far with what you described in your Goal Document. What matches? What's different? What's missing?

**6. Commit your work to your portfolio.** Use a descriptive commit message that says what you accomplished.

**7. Reminder:** The artifact package is due next week (Week 10). It includes three things: the artifact itself, process documentation (in your own words), and evidence of sharing (e.g., a peer reviewed it).
