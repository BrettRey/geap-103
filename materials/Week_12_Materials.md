# GEAP 103 Week 12: Instructor Materials Package

**Title:** Is It Working?
**Phase:** Creation (Week 12 of 14)
**Contact time:** 3 x 55 min (165 min total: 150 min instruction + 15 min break)
**Date prepared:** 2026-03-28

This file contains everything you need to prepare slides and run the class for Week 12. The lesson plan (`lesson-plans/week-12.md`) has the pedagogical rationale. This file has the concrete content. In case of conflict between this materials package and the lesson plan for Week 12, this materials package governs (it reflects March 2026 design revisions). For anything not covered here, the lesson plan governs.

**Week 12 design note:** Two things happen this week: Git Episodes are due (10% of final grade, checklist-based) and students prepare for the Week 13 oral reflection by mapping their portfolio evidence to CLOs. The session review (defending a DL self-assessment to a sceptical AI instructor) directly rehearses the oral reflection. Collaborative building continues with rotated roles. The Goal Document asks the most linguistically demanding comparison of the semester: Week 4 vs. now.

**No new vocabulary.** Consolidation of all 40 terms from Weeks 1--9. Key recycled terms: all assessment vocabulary (verify, evaluate, process, outcome) and all Git terms (commit, push, pull request, repository, restore, branch).

**AI tools:** Same tool-agnostic approach. AI assists during collaborative building and provides the interlocutor role during the session review. During the evidence mapping, students work independently (no AI).

## Segment A: Portfolio Curation + Git Episodes (0:00--0:40)

### Git Episodes Submission + Check (0:00--0:10)

**Format:** Individual, then whole class

> "Git Episodes are due today. Three episodes: Restore, Error Recovery, Collaboration. Each needs the story (what happened) and the evidence (screenshot or log)."

Students open their Git Episodes evidence. Display the self-check:

> **Git Episodes -- check your evidence now:**
> - [ ] **Restore:** recovered an earlier version (screenshot or log of revert/checkout/restore)
> - [ ] **Error Recovery:** something broke, you diagnosed it, you fixed it (error message + fix)
> - [ ] **Collaboration:** a PR with description + one comment exchange (screenshot or link)
>
> If you're missing one, you have until end of class. If you're missing two, come see me.

Students who are complete: submit now through the established process.

Students missing episodes: use collaborative building time (Segment B) to generate evidence. The Collaboration episode is the easiest to generate today -- a PR with description + comment exchange during group work counts.

**Contingency for the Collaboration episode:** If a student needs this episode but their group partner is unavailable, pair them with another student who also needs the episode. They can create a quick cross-group PR exchange.

---

### Worked Example: Mapping Evidence to CLOs (0:10--0:30)

**Format:** Instructor demonstration

> "Next week is the oral reflection. You'll have a 4-minute conversation with me about your learning. I'll ask about 2 CLOs. You point to evidence and tell me how strong it is. Today you prepare."

Display the 7 CLOs:

> | CLO | What it says (simplified) |
> |-----|--------------------------|
> | 1 | Manage files and versions |
> | 2 | Troubleshoot |
> | 3 | Guide AI tools |
> | 4 | Evaluate AI text |
> | 5 | Communicate |
> | 6 | Produce digital products |
> | 7 | Collaborate |

Model the process for one CLO (strong example):

> **CLO 4: Evaluate AI text**
> *Claim:* "I can identify when AI output is wrong and explain why."
> *Evidence:* Decision Log entry 5 -- I rejected Copilot's chart because it used the wrong data range. I noticed because the totals didn't match my manual calculation.
> *Strength:* "This is strong evidence because it shows I checked independently, not just accepted it."
> *Growth area:* "I'm better at catching number errors than text errors."

> "That's what the conversation sounds like. You make a claim, point to evidence, and evaluate how strong it is. Honest is better than impressive."

Then model a weak example:

> **CLO 1: Manage files and versions**
> *Claim:* "I can manage files."
> *Evidence:* "I have a folder."
> *Strength:* "It's good."

> "What's wrong with this? The claim is vague, the evidence is thin, and the evaluation says nothing. Compare that with: 'My Week 9 commit history shows 7 weeks of consistent descriptive file names and folder paths. That's stronger evidence than the Week 4 folder screenshot, which just shows I organised once.'"

### Students Map Evidence for 3--4 CLOs (0:30--0:40)

**Format:** Individual work, then structured A/B pair check

Students create an evidence map using the template:

> | CLO | Best evidence | Where to find it | Strong or weak? |
> |-----|--------------|------------------|-----------------|
> | 1 | ... | ... | ... |
> | 2 | ... | ... | ... |
> | 3 | ... | ... | ... |
> | 4 | ... | ... | ... |

**Writing** (7 min): Map at least 3--4 CLOs.

**Pair check with A/B turns** (3 min): "Partner A: tell me your strongest CLO and your evidence. Partner B: is that convincing? Then switch."

**What to say while circulating:**

| What you see | What to say |
|-------------|------------|
| Student with empty evidence map | "Start with the CLO you feel strongest about. What did you make? Where is it?" |
| Student listing evidence without evaluation | "You've listed what you did. Now: is that strong evidence or weak evidence? Why?" |
| Student who can't find evidence for a CLO | "That's useful to know. If you have weak evidence for a CLO, say so honestly in the reflection. That's more impressive than pretending." |
| Student who mapped all 7 CLOs | "Good. Now rank them: which is your strongest? Which is your weakest?" |

## Segment B: Collaborative Building + Roles (0:40--1:35)

### Role Rotation + Planning (0:40--0:45)

**Format:** Small groups

> "Roles rotate this week. Last week's Coordinator becomes this week's Builder (or Reviewer). Everyone gets a different perspective."

Groups confirm new roles and plan the session:
- **Coordinator:** What Issues are open? What's the priority for today?
- **Builder:** What artifact work remains? What's the most important thing to build?
- **Reviewer:** What hasn't been reviewed yet? What quality checks are needed?

---

### Collaborative Building (0:45--1:25)

**Format:** Small groups with instructor circulation

Groups build. Same role-based structure as Week 11 but with rotated roles.

**AI reflection pause at 1:05** (2 min):

> "Stop building. Write one sentence: what did you just ask AI to do, and what did you decide about the result?"

**Git Episodes -- last chance:** Students missing the Collaboration episode should submit a PR with description + comment exchange during this building time.

> "If you need a Collaboration episode, do it now. Submit a PR, get a comment from your partner, save the screenshot."

**What to say while circulating:**

| What you see | What to say |
|-------------|------------|
| Student hasn't submitted Git Episodes | "Have you submitted your three episodes? If not, which one are you missing?" |
| Student unclear on new role | "What's your new role? How is it different from last week?" |
| Group near completion | "What has your group accomplished? What's the plan for finishing by next week?" |
| PR descriptions are vague | "Read your PR description. Could someone who wasn't here understand what you changed?" |
| Student needs Collaboration episode | "Submit a PR now with a clear description. Ask your partner to comment. Save the screenshot. That's your episode." |
| Group stuck or off track | "Coordinator: what Issue are you working on? What's the next step?" |

## Segment C: Goal Document + Session Review + Closing (1:50--2:45)

### Goal Document: Comparing Week 4 with Now (1:50--2:10)

**Format:** Individual reading, then writing

> "Go back to your Week 4 Goal Document entry -- the one where you took stock after Phase 1. Read it. Then write about what changed."

**Reading** (5 min): Students read their Week 4 entry (and optionally skim Weeks 1--3).

**Prompt** (display):

> Compare what you wrote in Week 4 with where you are now. What changed? Why?

**Sentence frames** *(optional -- use if you're stuck)*:

> - "In Week 4, I planned to _____. Now I am _____."
> - "The biggest change is _____ because _____."
> - "I didn't expect _____, but it turned out that _____."
> - "Although I thought _____, what actually happened is _____."

**Writing** (15 min): Students write entry 12 in their Goal Document. This requires temporal comparison: looking back 8 weeks and articulating what changed and why. This is the most linguistically demanding comparison the Goal Document has asked for.

### Bridging Activity (2:10--2:12)

**Format:** Individual, silent

> "In two minutes, you'll defend one of your Decision Log self-assessments to AI. AI is playing a sceptical instructor. Think: which DL entry did you rate yourself highest on? What would you say if I asked 'Why did you rate that Excellent?'"

---

### Session Review (2:12--2:25)

**Format:** Individual, AI voice mode

**Role-setting prompt** (on slide):

> Speaking practice. I'm a B1 English student practising explaining my work. Ask me follow-up questions. If my explanation is vague, tell me what's unclear and ask me to be more specific. Don't do the explaining for me.

**This week's scenario** (on slide):

> AI plays an instructor asking about one of your Decision Log entries. Defend your self-assessment rating. Why did you rate it the way you did?

AI role: Sceptical instructor.

Students talk to AI for 10--12 minutes. The AI challenges their self-assessment: "You rated this Excellent. What makes it Excellent and not just Proficient?" "What evidence supports your rating?"

This directly rehearses the oral reflection (Week 13), where the instructor will ask similar questions.

**Self-check** (1 min):

> "Write two sentences: What rating did you defend? What was the hardest question AI asked?"

**Note:** Voice mode starts a new chat.

### Week 13 Preparation (2:25--2:35)

**Format:** Whole class

> "Next week is the oral reflection. Here's what happens."

Display:

> **Oral Reflection (Week 13)**
> - 4 minutes, one-on-one with the instructor
> - Your portfolio is open with evidence pre-tagged
> - I pick 2 CLOs to discuss (not all 7)
> - You make a claim ("I can now..."), point to evidence, and evaluate how strong it is
> - I ask one follow-up question per CLO
>
> **Before the conversation starts:** You'll have 5 minutes to tag your portfolio (bookmark the evidence you'd point to for 3--4 CLOs)
>
> **While you wait:** You'll work on a finalization checklist for your final artifact package (Week 14)

> "The evidence mapping you did today is your preparation. Review it before next class. Know where your strongest evidence is for each CLO."

## Catch-Up Instructions for Latecomers

### If You Missed Week 12

In Week 12, we submitted Git Episodes, mapped portfolio evidence to CLOs, continued collaborative building with rotated roles, wrote a Goal Document comparing Week 4 with now, and practised defending a DL self-assessment in the session review.

**1. Submit your Git Episodes immediately.** Three required episodes:

| Episode | Evidence required |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Restore** | Recovered an earlier version (screenshot or log of revert/checkout/restore) |
| **Error Recovery** | Something broke, you diagnosed it, you fixed it (error message + fix) |
| **Collaboration** | A PR with description + one comment exchange (screenshot or link) |

Contact the instructor about a late deadline. This is 10% of your grade.

**2. Create your evidence map.** For 3--4 CLOs, identify:

| CLO | Best evidence | Where to find it | Strong or weak? |
|-----|--------------|------------------|-----------------|

Use the simplified CLO table: (1) Manage files and versions, (2) Troubleshoot, (3) Guide AI tools, (4) Evaluate AI text, (5) Communicate, (6) Produce digital products, (7) Collaborate.

You need this for the Week 13 oral reflection.

**3. Update your Goal Document.** Write entry 12: Compare what you wrote in Week 4 with where you are now. What changed? Why?

**4. Prepare for the oral reflection (Week 13).** You will have a 4-minute one-on-one conversation with the instructor. You pick one CLO you feel strongest about, show the evidence, and evaluate how strong it is. The instructor picks a second CLO. Your evidence map is your preparation.

**5. Check your collaborative role.** Roles rotated this week. Ask your group what your new role is.
