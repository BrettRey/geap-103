# GEAP 103 Week 13: Instructor Materials Package

**Title:** The Conversation
**Phase:** Creation (Week 13 of 14) -- Assessment Week
**Contact time:** 3 x 55 min (165 min total: 150 min instruction + 15 min break)
**Date prepared:** 2026-03-28

This file contains everything you need to prepare slides and run the class for Week 13. The lesson plan (`lesson-plans/week-13.md`) has the pedagogical rationale. This file has the concrete content. In case of conflict between this materials package and the lesson plan for Week 13, this materials package governs (it reflects March 2026 design revisions). For anything not covered here, the lesson plan governs.

**Week 13 design note:** This is the centrepiece assessment of the course's metacognitive thread. The oral reflection (10% of final grade) is the culminating unmediated spoken performance. Students have been practising explaining their work to AI every week since Week 1. By Week 13, explaining work orally is a well-practised skill, not a novel demand. The session review from Week 12 (defending a DL self-assessment to a sceptical instructor) was the direct rehearsal. Three other things happen alongside: the collaborative artifact is submitted with contribution statements, the final Goal Document entry traces change across 13 weeks, and students prepare for the Week 14 sharing event.

**No new vocabulary.** All 40 terms from Weeks 1--9 deployed naturally in oral reflection, contribution statements, and Goal Document writing. The oral reflection is the best single indicator of whether vocabulary has been acquired to productive level.

**No session review this week.** The oral reflection IS the reflection activity.

**AI tools:** No AI during the oral reflection (unmediated). AI may be used during collaborative finalization and Week 14 preparation.

### Pre-Class Preparation

- Set up the oral reflection station: a desk or corner away from the main group, with a visible timer and the scoring rubric ready.
- For each student, identify 2 CLOs to discuss (from reading their DLs, evidence maps, and Goal Documents). One should be a likely strength; one should be a likely weaker area.
- Prepare one follow-up question per CLO per student. These can be generic ("What would you do differently?") but specific questions are better ("Your DL entry 6 rated Excellent -- what makes you say that?").
- Know your schedule. At 5 minutes per student (4 min conversation + 1 min transition):

| Class size | Reflection time needed | Remaining for structured work |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| 15 | 75 min | 70 min |
| 20 | 100 min | 45 min |
| 24 | 120 min | 25 min |

- Plan the call order. Students with partial/deferred observations from earlier rounds go first.
- Confirm collaborative artifact submission process (commit to shared repository or other established method).
- Review group repositories: are groups near completion? Flag groups that need intervention.

## Segment A: Pre-Tagging + Oral Reflections Begin (0:00--0:40)

### Framing (0:00--0:05)

**Format:** Whole class

> "Today I talk to each of you one-on-one for 4 minutes about your learning. This is the oral reflection, 10% of your grade. While you wait, you'll finalize your collaborative artifact and write your last Goal Document entry."

> "The conversation goes like this: I ask you to pick one CLO you feel strongest about. You show me the evidence. You tell me how strong it is. Then I pick a second CLO and you do the same. Four minutes total."

---

### Pre-Tagging (0:05--0:10)

**Format:** Individual (whole class, simultaneous)

> "Open your portfolio. You have 5 minutes to tag your evidence. For 3--4 CLOs, open or bookmark the specific artifact you would point to. Use your evidence map from last week."

Students tag their portfolio: open Decision Log entries, artifact packages, Git history, PRs, or Goal Document entries that serve as evidence for specific CLOs. This reduces navigation friction during the conversation.

Display:

> **Pre-tagging checklist:**
> - [ ] Open your evidence map from Week 12
> - [ ] For 3--4 CLOs: bookmark the artifact you'd point to
> - [ ] Know which CLO is your strongest
> - [ ] Know which CLO you're weakest on (I might ask about it)

---

### Oral Reflections Begin + Structured Work (0:10--0:40)

**Format:** One-on-one conversations at the reflection station; individual/group work for the rest of the class

The instructor begins calling students one at a time. Reflections run continuously through Segments A, B, and C until all students are done.

**Oral reflection process (per student):**

1. Student sits down at the reflection station. Portfolio is open.
2. Instructor: "Pick one CLO you feel strongest about. Show me the evidence."
3. Student makes a claim ("I can now..."), points to a pre-tagged artifact, evaluates the evidence ("This is strong because..." or "This is OK but I wish I had...").
4. Instructor asks one follow-up question.
5. Instructor selects a second CLO (typically one the student might be weaker on).
6. Student responds with claim, evidence, and evaluation.
7. Hard stop at 4 minutes. Use a visible timer.

**This is unmediated.** No AI assistance. The student's portfolio is open for reference, but the language is entirely their own.

**If a student freezes:** "Look at your Decision Logs. Which entry are you most proud of? Tell me about it."

**Scoring rubric (score during or immediately after each conversation):**

> **GEAP 103 -- Oral Evidence-Indexed Reflection Scoring Rubric (Week 13)**
>
> **Student name:** ______________________ **Date:** _______________
>
> **CLO 1 discussed (student's choice):** _______________
>
> **CLO 2 discussed (instructor's choice):** _______________
>
> ---
>
> | Level | Description |
> |-------|-------------|
> | **Excellent** | Specific claims about 2 CLOs; points to appropriate evidence; honest evaluation of evidence strength; genuine growth areas identified; can answer follow-ups with detail |
> | **Proficient** | Claims present for 2 CLOs; evidence identified; some evaluation of quality; adequate follow-up answers |
> | **Developing** | General claims; vague evidence links; no evaluation of quality; struggles with follow-ups |
> | **Insufficient** | Cannot articulate claims; no evidence identified; cannot engage in conversation |
>
> **Score:** [ ] Excellent [ ] Proficient [ ] Developing [ ] Insufficient
>

>

>

**Structured work while waiting** (display):

> **While you wait for your reflection, work through this finalization checklist:**
>
> 1. **Collaborative artifact:** Run Accessibility Checker on the final version. Fix any issues.
> 2. **Process documentation:** Complete your process documentation for the collaborative project (what AI did, what you changed, what you checked). In your own words.
> 3. **Peer response notes:** Have you incorporated feedback from the cross-group review?
> 4. **Sharing preparation:** Prepare for Week 14. What will you show? What will you say? Write a 1-minute explanation of one design decision you made.
>
> *This checklist is a deliverable: submit it (checked off, with notes) by end of session. It is ungraded but provides structure and accountability.*

**What to say when checking in with waiting students:**

| What you see | What to say |
|-------------|------------|
| Student sitting idle | "Have you finished all four items on the checklist? Show me." |
| Student unsure about Accessibility Checker | "Open the collaborative artifact. In Word: Review tab, Check Accessibility. In Google Docs: Tools, Accessibility." |
| Student writing process documentation vaguely | "Be specific: which AI tool, what did you ask for, what did you change in the result?" |
| Student not preparing for Week 14 | "Write a 1-minute explanation of one design decision. What did your group decide to do, and why?" |

## Segment B: Reflections Continue + Collaborative Artifact Finalization (0:40--1:35)

### Oral Reflections Continue + Collaborative Work (0:40--1:25)

**Format:** Same as Segment A. Instructor conducts reflections at the station; students work.

Groups finalize the collaborative artifact. Each student works within their role:

- **Coordinator:** Checks all Issues are resolved or closed. Writes a brief project summary.
- **Builder:** Completes any remaining production. Ensures the artifact is functional.
- **Reviewer:** Final review pass. Runs Accessibility Checker. Writes or updates verification notes.

---

### Commit + Submit Collaborative Artifact (1:25--1:35)

**Format:** Group + individual

> "Collaborative artifact deadline. Commit and submit now."

Groups commit and submit their collaborative artifact through the established process.

**Contribution statements** (each student writes individually, 2--3 sentences):

Display:

> **Write your contribution statement (2--3 sentences):**
> - What did you build or produce?
> - What PRs did you submit and review?
> - What did you contribute to the group's planning and quality?
>
> "This is checked against your Git history. Be accurate."

Each student submits their individual contribution statement alongside the group submission.

## Segment C: Reflections Continue + Final Goal Document (1:50--2:45)

### Oral Reflections Continue (if needed) + Final Goal Document (1:50--2:15)

**Format:** Individual reading and writing (while reflections continue if needed)

For students not currently in a reflection:

> "This is your last Goal Document entry. Go back to the beginning. Read your Week 1 entry. Read every entry after that. Notice how your language changed. Notice how your thinking changed. Then write about what you see."

**Step 1 -- Read** (10 min): Students read their entire Goal Document from Week 1 to Week 12. No writing yet.

**Step 2 -- Write** (15 min):

**Prompt** (display):

> Read your Goal Document from the beginning. Write a final entry that answers: What did I learn about what I want? How did my goals change? What can I do now that I couldn't do in Week 1?

**Sentence frames** *(optional -- use if you're stuck)*:

> - "When I started, I thought _____. Now I think _____."
> - "The most important thing I learned is _____ because _____."
> - "My goals changed from _____ to _____ because _____."
> - "I can now _____, which I couldn't do before because _____."

**Instructor note:** This is the culminating language moment of the Goal Document arc. The transformation in articulation from Week 1 to now IS the course. Students who wrote "I wish my computer could..." in Week 1 now write about what they can do and how their thinking changed.

---

### Commit Final Goal Document (2:15--2:20)

**Format:** Individual

> "Commit your final Goal Document entry. 13 entries across 13 weeks."

---

### Week 14 Preparation (2:20--2:35)

**Format:** Individual, then pairs

> "Next week is the last class. You'll share what you built, individually and as a group. Let's prepare."

**Individual** (7 min): Plan your informal presentation:
- What will you show? (Your artifact, your portfolio, your favourite DL entry)
- What will you say about what you learned?
- Is this what you set out to make? What changed?

**Pair rehearsal with A/B turns** (8 min): Partner A presents for 2 minutes, Partner B asks one question. Switch.

> "Did your partner's explanation make sense? What was the strongest moment?"

**Group check** (if time): Groups briefly confirm who introduces (Coordinator), who demonstrates (Builder), who evaluates (Reviewer) for the Week 14 group presentation.

---

### Closing (2:35--2:45)

**Format:** Whole class

> "Today I heard each of you talk about your learning. That conversation, making claims, pointing to evidence, evaluating honestly, is a skill that transfers to every course and every job."
>
> "Your collaborative artifact is submitted. Your Goal Document has 13 entries. Next week: final submissions and sharing."
>
> "Next week you submit: (1) Final Artifact Package (product + documentation + verification + sharing evidence), (2) Evidence-Indexed Reflection is done (that was today's conversation). Come ready to show what you built and say what you learned."
>
> "I'll read your Goal Documents this week, all 13 entries, and return brief final comments."

## Catch-Up Instructions for Latecomers

### If You Missed Week 13

In Week 13, the instructor conducted one-on-one oral reflections (4 min each, 10% of your grade), groups submitted the collaborative artifact with contribution statements, and students wrote the final Goal Document entry (entry 13).

**1. Schedule your oral reflection immediately.** This is 10% of your grade. Contact the instructor to arrange a make-up during Week 14 (before or after the sharing event, or during office hours). Bring your portfolio with evidence pre-tagged for 3--4 CLOs. The format:

| Step | What happens |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | You pick one CLO you feel strongest about. Show the evidence. Evaluate how strong it is. |
| 2 | The instructor picks a second CLO. You do the same. |
| 3 | The instructor asks follow-up questions. 4 minutes total. |

Use your evidence map from Week 12 to prepare.

**2. Submit your contribution statement.** Write 2--3 sentences:
- What did you build or produce?
- What PRs did you submit and review?
- What did you contribute to the group's planning and quality?

This is checked against your Git history. Be accurate. Submit through the established process.

**3. Check that your group submitted the collaborative artifact.** If your group couldn't submit without you, contact them and the instructor immediately.

**4. Write your final Goal Document entry (entry 13).** Read your entire Goal Document from Week 1. Then write a final entry: What did I learn about what I want? How did my goals change? What can I do now that I couldn't do in Week 1? Commit it.

**5. Prepare for Week 14.** Next week is the sharing event and final artifact package submission. Plan what you will show and what you will say about what you learned.
