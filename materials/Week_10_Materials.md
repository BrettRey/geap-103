# GEAP 103 Week 10: Instructor Materials Package

**Title:** Building My Thing (Part 2) + Submission
**Phase:** Building (Week 10 of 14) -- final week of Phase 2
**Contact time:** 3 x 55 min (165 min total: 150 min instruction + 15 min break)
**Date prepared:** 2026-03-28

This file contains everything you need to prepare slides and run the class for Week 10. The lesson plan (`lesson-plans/week-10.md`) has the pedagogical rationale. This file has the concrete content. In case of conflict between this materials package and the lesson plan for Week 10, this materials package governs (it reflects March 2026 design revisions). For anything not covered here, the lesson plan governs.

**Week 10 design note:** Three things happen this week: submission (mid-semester artifact package due), reflection (the most linguistically demanding Goal Document entry so far), and transition (collaborative project formation for Phase 3). The session review comes before the written reflection -- students present their artifact to AI as a client, which primes the reflective writing. This is the last week of Phase 2. Starting Week 11, the question shifts from "What do I want?" to "What do we want?"

**No new vocabulary.** Consolidation of all 40 terms from Weeks 1–9 as students use relevant vocabulary in artifact work, process documentation, verification notes, and session review.

**AI tools:** Same tool-agnostic approach. AI is used during project finalisation (fixing, formatting, checking). During the session review, AI plays a client who commissioned the work -- the first non-technical audience scenario.

## Segment A: Verification and Quality Check (0:00–0:40)

### Framing (0:00–0:05)

> "Today you finish and submit your mid-semester artifact package. The package is three things: what you built, documentation of how you built it in your own words, and evidence of sharing. But before you submit, you check your work. Not just 'does it look OK?' but 'did I build what I said I would?'"

---

### Worked Example: The Gap Between Plan and Result (0:05–0:25)

Instructor demonstrates the full final review process using their own (or a sample) artifact.

**Step 1 -- Re-read the Goal Document.** Display a Goal Document entry (the project plan) and the finished artifact side by side.

> "Here's what I said I would build. Here's what I actually built. Let's compare."

**Step 2 -- Name the gap honestly.** Think aloud:

> "I planned to include three charts, but I only finished two. The third was too complicated -- I couldn't get Copilot to generate the right format, and I ran out of time. That's not a failure. That's useful information. The honest thing to write is: 'I planned three charts but completed two because the third required a chart type I couldn't figure out in the time available.'"

**Step 3 -- Run the Accessibility Checker.** Run the checker on the artifact. Fix one issue on screen (e.g., missing alt text).

> "This is part of verification. You check that what you built is usable, not just finished."

**Step 4 -- Write the verification note.** Write on the projector:

> "I checked my spreadsheet with the Accessibility Checker. It found one image without alt text, which I added. I also tested the formulas by entering sample data and comparing the results with a calculator. The budget totals match. I re-read my Goal Document from Week 8 and confirmed that I built the budget tracker I described, except for the savings projection chart, which I did not complete."

> "This is in my own words. It says what I checked, what I found, and what I didn't finish. If this sounds very different from how I talk, that's a red flag."

### Artifact Package Requirements Review (0:25–0:40)

Display the artifact package components:

> | Component | What to submit | Notes |
> |-----------|---------------|-------|
> | **Product** | The artifact itself | Must be functional. Accessibility features where applicable. |
> | **Process documentation** | Written in your own words: what AI did, what you changed and why, what you checked | Not AI-generated prose. Use your process table from Week 9. |
> | **Verification note** | What you checked, what you found, what you fixed, what you didn't finish | Honest. Includes the gap between plan and result. |
> | **Evidence of sharing** | How you explained or showed your work to someone else | At least one documented instance. Week 9 peer check-in counts. Session review transcript also counts. |

> "Questions? **Deadline: submit by end of Segment B (1:35). If you need more time, submit by 11:59 PM tonight.** If your artifact isn't perfect, that's fine. The documentation matters as much as the product."

**Evidence of sharing -- minimum threshold:** At least one documented instance. Students who missed Week 9 can satisfy this by saving their session review transcript (AI-as-client) or explaining their artifact to a partner during collaborative formation.

## Segment B: Planning + Finalisation + Submission (0:40–1:35)

### Pre-Task Planning (0:40–0:45)

> "Open your Goal Document. Re-read your project plan from Weeks 7–8 and your task list from Week 9. Check your process documentation table. What's done? What's not? What do you need to finish in the next 35 minutes?"

Students write a brief priority list:
- What is complete
- What still needs to be done
- What they will skip if they run out of time

---

### Finalisation and Submission (0:45–1:20)

Students work on three tasks in whatever order makes sense:

1. **Finish the artifact.** Complete any remaining work on the product.
2. **Write process documentation.** In their own words: what AI did, what they changed, what they checked. Students who used the process table from Week 9 build on it. Students who didn't: reconstruct from memory and their Decision Log entries.
3. **Write the verification note.** Run the Accessibility Checker (if applicable). Compare the artifact against the Goal Document plan. Write what was checked, what was found, and what was not completed.

**What to say while circulating:**

| What you see | What to say |
|-------------|------------|
| Student unsure where to put files or how to submit | Help them compile and submit. Walk through the submission process. |
| Process documentation sounds unlike the student's spoken English | "Can you read me your process documentation? Does it sound like how you'd explain this to me in person?" If it sounds markedly unlike the student's speech, prompt them to rewrite in simpler language. |
| Student avoiding writing about what they didn't finish | "What didn't you finish? Write that down. Naming what you didn't do is part of the documentation." |
| Student done early | "Begin reading your full Goal Document from Week 1. You'll need it for the reflection after the break." |

---

### Commit and Submit (1:20–1:35)

Students commit their complete artifact package to their portfolio repository. Then submit through the established submission process (OneDrive folder, LMS upload, or GitHub link).

Commit message:

> "Submitted mid-semester artifact package: [brief description of artifact] + process documentation + verification note."

Students who finish early: begin reading their full Goal Document from Week 1 (preparation for the reflection in Segment C).

## Segment C: Session Review + Reflection + Collaborative Formation (1:50–2:45)

### Bridging Activity (1:50–1:52)

> "You just submitted your artifact. In two minutes, you'll present it to AI as if AI were a client who paid for the work. Think about: what does it do? Who is it for? What's one decision you made during the process?"

Two minutes of silence. Students think and prepare.

---

### Session Review -- Speaking Practice (1:52–2:04)

Display:

> **Open your AI tool. Start a new chat.**
>
> **First, say this (or paste it):**
> "Speaking practice. I'm a B1 English student practising explaining my work. Ask me follow-up questions. If my explanation is vague, tell me what's unclear and ask me to be more specific. Don't do the explaining for me."
>
> **Then, your task:** Present your mid-semester artifact to AI as if it were a client. Explain what it does, who it's for, and one decision you made during the process.

AI role: Client who commissioned the work.

Students talk to AI for 10–12 minutes. The AI asks follow-up questions as a client would: "Why did you choose this approach?" "What would you change if you had more time?" "Who exactly is this for?"

This is the first scenario where the audience is non-technical. Previous weeks practised explaining to peers, teammates, and help desks. A client audience requires simplifying technical details and foregrounding purpose over process.

**Note:** Voice mode starts a new chat. The prompt is said aloud or pasted at the start.

**What to listen for while circulating:**

| What you hear | What it means |
|--------------|---------------|
| Student describes artifact purpose clearly to a non-technical audience | Good production -- the simplification skill is developing |
| Student defaults to process details instead of purpose | Nudge: "The client doesn't care how you built it. They care what it does for them." |
| Student is silent | Approach quietly: "Start with one sentence: what does your artifact do?" |
| Student and AI are having a genuine back-and-forth | Let it run. This is the goal. |

**Self-check** (1 min):

> "Write two sentences: What did the 'client' want to know most? What was hardest to explain?"

---

### Goal Document: Reflection on the Gap (2:04–2:16)

> "You just presented your artifact to a client. Now write about the gap between what you planned and what you built. What you said to AI can help you -- you've just practised articulating it."

**Prompt** (display):

> Compare what you planned with what you built. Write 3–4 sentences about the gap.

**Sentence frames** *(optional -- use if you're stuck)*:

> - "I set out to build _____. What I actually built is _____."
> - "The biggest difference is _____ because _____."
> - "If I had more time, I would change _____."
> - "I learned that _____ is harder/easier than I expected because _____."

**Writing** (12 min): Students write entry 10 in their Goal Document. The session review primes this: students have just articulated the gap orally and can now write a more considered version.

**Instructor note:** This is a Phase 3 language moment. Students are moving from "I will..." to "Although I planned..., what I actually..." This requires subordination, hedging, and concession -- features that B1 learners are still developing. The sentence frames scaffold this, but monitor whether stronger students are moving beyond them.

---

### Collaborative Project Formation (2:16–2:35)

> "You've built something on your own. For the last four weeks, you'll build something with a partner or small group. This is a new thing, not a continuation of your individual project."

**Step 1 -- Interest inventory** (3 min): Each student writes on a card or shared document:

> - "I'm interested in _____."
> - "I'm good at _____."
> - "I want to learn _____."

**Step 2 -- Gallery walk** (8 min, default for classes of 15+): Cards posted on wall or shared slide. Students circulate, read all cards, and note 2-3 people whose interests or skills complement theirs. *(For small classes under 15, a round-robin works: each student shares aloud, 30 seconds each.)*

**Step 3 -- Group formation** (9 min): Instructor facilitates pairing/grouping based on shared interests or complementary skills. Groups of 2–3. Students do not need to share the same project type; complementary skills are valuable.

Once groups form, they begin discussing: "What do *we* want to build together?" This is the beginning, not a commitment.

**What to say while circulating:**

| What you see | What to say |
|-------------|------------|
| Student has no idea what to write on the card | "Think about what you enjoyed building this semester. What tool or project type was interesting?" |
| Two students with matching interests | Point them toward each other: "You both mentioned _____. Talk." |
| Student left out of groups | Facilitate directly: place them with a group that has complementary needs. Plan to follow up in Week 11. |
| Group already debating project ideas | Good. Let it run. Remind: "This is the beginning, not a commitment." |

---

*DL feedback round 2 is returned at the start of Week 11, not today. Students have zero bandwidth for reading evaluative feedback after submitting, presenting, reflecting, and forming groups.*

>

## Catch-Up Instructions for Latecomers

### If You Missed Week 10

In Week 10, we submitted the mid-semester artifact package, reflected on the gap between our plans and our results, and formed collaborative project groups for Phase 3.

**1. Submit your mid-semester artifact package.** The package has four components:

| Component | What to submit |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Product** | The artifact itself (must be functional, accessibility features where applicable) |
| **Process documentation** | In your own words: what AI did, what you changed and why, what you checked |
| **Verification note** | What you checked, what you found, what you fixed, what you didn't finish |
| **Evidence of sharing** | How you explained or showed your work to someone else (Week 9 peer check-in counts) |

Submit through the established process (OneDrive folder, LMS upload, or GitHub link). Contact the instructor about a late submission deadline.

**2. Write your verification note.** Run the Accessibility Checker on your artifact (if applicable). Compare your artifact against your Goal Document plan (Weeks 7–8 entries). Write what you checked, what you found, and what you did not complete.

**3. Update your Goal Document.** Write entry 10: Compare what you planned with what you built. Write 3–4 sentences about the gap. Use these frames if you're stuck:
- "I set out to build _____. What I actually built is _____."
- "The biggest difference is _____ because _____."
- "If I had more time, I would change _____."

**4. Complete the interest inventory.** Write three statements on a card or shared document:
- "I'm interested in _____."
- "I'm good at _____."
- "I want to learn _____."

Bring this to Week 11. The instructor will place you in a collaborative group.

**5. Read your DL feedback.** Decision Log feedback round 2 (entries 3–5) was returned. Compare it with your Round 1 feedback from Week 6. Your remaining entries (6–8) are assessed summatively.

**6. Commit your work.** Commit your artifact package to your portfolio repository with a descriptive message.
