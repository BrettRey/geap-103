# GEAP 103 Week 12 Lesson Plan: Is It Working?

**Context:** 3 x 55 min (150 min instruction + 15-min break), B1 learners, BYOD, Office 365 + Copilot
**Week 12 of 14** | Phase 3: Creation (assessment week)

**Learning Objectives (Week 12):**
1. Map portfolio evidence to CLOs and evaluate the strength of that evidence (metacognitive, evaluative language)
2. Demonstrate oral explanation of technical work in Micro-Defence 3 (CLOs 2, 5 assessed)
3. Give and receive cross-group structured peer feedback on Goal Document progression
4. Write collaboratively and individually about progress, comparing current work with earlier intentions (subordination, hedging, temporal comparison)

**No new vocabulary.** Consolidation. All 40 terms from Weeks 1-9 are now fully in play.

**Goal Thread -- Progress Report:**
This is the third and final assessment week. Students have been writing Goal Document entries for 11 weeks. The peer response this week is cross-group (not within the group, as in Weeks 4 and 8), which means a reader who doesn't know the project intimately must follow the progression. This is a higher communicative bar: the reader can't fill in gaps from shared context. The individual Goal Document entry asks students to compare Week 4 with now, requiring temporal comparison ("In Week 4, I planned to... Now I am..."), subordination ("Although I expected..."), and evaluative language ("The biggest change is... because..."). This is the most linguistically demanding comparison the Goal Document has asked for.

## Segment A: Portfolio Curation Preview (0:00--0:40)

### 0:00--0:05 | Framing
**Format:** Whole class
**Language skills:** Listening

"Today is an assessment week. Same pattern as Weeks 4 and 8: written diagnostic, oral micro-defence, peer feedback. But today there's something extra: you're going to start mapping your evidence to the CLOs. In two weeks, you submit your Evidence-Indexed Reflection. Today you start thinking about what evidence you have and how strong it is."

"Roles continue. The Coordinator updates Issues. The Builder continues production. The Reviewer checks quality. Between micro-defences and the new activities, you'll have time to keep building."

### 0:05--0:25 | Worked Example: Mapping Evidence to CLOs
**Format:** Instructor demonstration
**Language skills:** Listening, reading (CLO descriptions, sample evidence)

Instructor demonstrates mapping evidence to CLOs using a sample portfolio (instructor's own or a fictional student's). Display the 7 CLOs on screen:

| CLO | What it says (simplified) |
|-----|--------------------------|
| 1 | Manage files and versions |
| 2 | Troubleshoot |
| 3 | Guide AI tools |
| 4 | Evaluate AI text |
| 5 | Communicate |
| 6 | Produce digital products |
| 7 | Collaborate |

**Step 1 -- Choose a CLO.** "Let's start with CLO 4: evaluate AI text. Where's my evidence? My Decision Logs. Let me find my strongest entry."

Instructor displays a sample Decision Log entry. "This one shows me rejecting AI output because it was inaccurate. I noticed the problem, explained why it was wrong, and described what I did instead. That's strong evidence for CLO 4."

**Step 2 -- Write an Evidence-Indexed Reflection entry.** Instructor models the format on screen:

> **CLO 4: Evaluate AI text**
> *Claim:* I can identify when AI output is wrong and explain why.
> *Evidence:* Decision Log entry 5 (Week 9) -- I rejected Copilot's chart because it used the wrong data range. I noticed because the totals didn't match my manual calculation.
> *Strength:* This is strong evidence because it shows I checked the output independently, not just accepted it.
> *Growth area:* I'm better at catching errors in spreadsheets than in documents. I need to practise checking AI-generated text more carefully.

"That's about 75 words. Do that for all 7 CLOs and you have your Evidence-Indexed Reflection. The key is: be specific and be honest. 'I did a lot of good work' is not evidence. 'Decision Log entry 5 shows I caught an error' is evidence."

### 0:25--0:35 | Students Identify Evidence for 2-3 CLOs
**Format:** Individual work
**Language skills:** Reading (own portfolio, CLO descriptions), writing (notes)

"Open your portfolio. Look through your Decision Logs, your artifact package, your Goal Document, your PRs and Issues. Pick 2-3 CLOs and find your strongest evidence for each. Write brief notes -- you'll use these in your Evidence-Indexed Reflection in Week 14."

Students work individually, reviewing their portfolio and matching evidence to CLOs. This is not a graded activity; it's preparation.

Instructor circulates: "Which CLO did you pick? What's your evidence? Is it strong or weak? How do you know?"

### 0:35--0:40 | Brief Pair Discussion
**Format:** Pairs (within groups)
**Language skills:** Speaking (explaining evidence choices), listening

"Tell your partner: which CLOs did you choose, and what evidence did you find? Is it strong?"

2-3 minutes. This gives students oral rehearsal of the evaluative language they'll need for the reflection.

## Segment B: Diagnostic + Micro-Defences + Project Work (0:40--1:35)

### 0:40--0:50 | Pre-Task Planning for Micro-Defence
**Format:** Individual reflection
**Language skills:** Reading (own portfolio, recent work)

Students review their full portfolio across 12 weeks. Prepare to explain one piece of work:
- "Choose one artifact from any point in the semester: a Decision Log entry, a PR you wrote, something you built, a troubleshooting episode, a collaboration note. Choose something you can explain well."
- "Think about: What did you do? What was the challenge? What tools did you use? What did you learn? What would you do differently?"

This is preparation to talk, not writing a script. Students organize their thoughts, open the artifact they want to show.

### 0:50--1:00 | In-Class Written Diagnostic
**Format:** Individual, unmediated (whole class, simultaneous)
**Language skills:** Writing (unmediated, ~50-100 words)

**Prompt** (displayed): "Choose one problem you solved this semester using a computer tool. Describe the problem, what you tried, and what worked. Use specific vocabulary from the course."

Rules:
- No AI assistance (Copilot closed, no chat tools)
- No notes, no partner
- Handwritten or on a locked-down device (instructor's call based on setup)
- 10 minutes

Collect when done. This provides unmediated written evidence of the student's independent language use (CLOs 2, 5). This is the third and final diagnostic; comparison with Weeks 4 and 8 diagnostics shows progression.

### 1:00--1:35 | Oral Micro-Defences Begin + Project Work
**Format:** Individual presentations (instructor + 1 peer) while others work
**Language skills:** Speaking (sustained technical explanation), listening (peer), evaluative language (peer feedback)

**~5-6 students** in this segment (at ~6 min each).

Per student:
1. Student walks through ONE artifact, explaining what they did, what the challenge was, and what they learned (3-4 min)
2. Instructor asks 1-2 follow-up questions (1 min)
3. One designated peer gives structured feedback: one strength and one question (1 min)

**Peer feedback guidance** (on screen for the peer): "Tell your partner one thing they explained clearly. Then ask one question about something you didn't understand or want to know more about."

Instructor uses the task checklist to score each defence:

| Criterion | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|
| Clear problem statement + context | check / partial / missing |
| Correct use of essential terminology | check / partial / missing |
| Shows what was tried (not just "it doesn't work") | check / partial / missing |
| Interprets instruction/error accurately | check / partial / missing |
| Justifies chosen solution | check / partial / missing |

**Scoring:** Oral fluency 30%, task component 50%, written diagnostic 10%, peer feedback 10%.

**While waiting:** Other students continue collaborative project work in their roles:
- **Coordinator:** Updates Issues, tracks progress, resolves any blockers
- **Builder:** Continues artifact production
- **Reviewer:** Reviews completed work, checks accessibility, writes verification notes

Students who are caught up on project work: begin the cross-group peer response on Goal Documents (below, can start early if micro-defence queue is long).

## Segment C: Micro-Defences Continue + Peer Response + Goal Document (1:50--2:45)

### 1:50--2:10 | Micro-Defences Continue
**Format:** Same as above

**~4-5 more students.** Others work on the activities below.

**Logistics note:** With 15-20 students at ~6 min each, most will finish in Week 12. Any remaining students complete their defence in the first 15-20 min of Week 13. Do not rush defences to fit the schedule; quality matters more than completion.

### 1:55--2:15 | Structured Peer Response on Goal Documents (Cross-Group)
**Format:** Pair work (cross-group: students from different project groups)
**Language skills:** Reading (another student's full Goal Document), writing (evaluative feedback), speaking (discussion)

*This activity runs in parallel with the remaining micro-defences. Students who have finished their defence join in.*

"This is the third time you've done peer response on Goal Documents. Week 4: your partner asked what interests you. Week 8: your partner asked whether your plan was clear enough to help with. Today: someone from a different group reads your Goal Document. They don't know your project. They can only follow your thinking if your writing is clear."

**Cross-group pairing:** Instructor pairs students from different groups. Each pair reads each other's full Goal Document (all 11 entries, Weeks 1-11).

**Response task** (displayed): Write answers to two questions about your partner's Goal Document:
1. "Is the progression from Week 1 to now clear? Can you follow how their thinking changed?"
2. "What's the strongest change in thinking? Point to a specific entry."

- Writing answers (5 min)
- Sharing with partner and discussing (10 min): "Here's what I noticed about how your thinking developed. Here's where it was hard to follow."
- Exchange written responses so each student has their cross-group peer's feedback

"This is a different kind of feedback. In Weeks 4 and 8, your peer knew your project. Today's reader doesn't. If they can follow your progression, your writing works. If they can't, you have revision to do before the Evidence-Indexed Reflection."

### 2:15--2:30 | Goal Document: Collaborative + Individual

**Part 1 -- Collaborative progress report (5 min).**
**Format:** Small groups (project groups)
**Language skills:** Writing (collaborative, evaluative), speaking (group negotiation)

"With your group, write a brief joint progress report. This goes in ONE group member's Goal Document (the Coordinator's) but everyone contributes."

**Prompt** (displayed): "What did we set out to build? What's done? What's left? What's the biggest problem we've solved?"

Groups write together. The Coordinator leads the writing. The Builder describes what's been built. The Reviewer evaluates what's working.

**Part 2 -- Individual Goal Document entry (10 min).**
**Format:** Individual writing
**Language skills:** Writing (temporal comparison, subordination, evaluative)

"Now write your own entry. This one is about YOU, not the group. Compare where you are now with where you were in Week 4."

**Prompt** (displayed): "Compare what you wrote in Week 4 with where you are now. What changed? Why?"

**Sentence frames** (displayed):
- "In Week 4, I planned to _____. Now I am _____."
- "The biggest change is _____ because _____."
- "I didn't expect _____, but it turned out that _____."

Students write (~10 min). This entry requires temporal comparison: looking back 8 weeks and articulating what changed and why. The subordination and hedging that this demands ("Although I thought...", "Even though I planned...", "I didn't expect...") are key B1 development targets.

### 2:30--2:35 | Decision Log Reminder
**Format:** Whole class (brief)

"Decision Logs are due next week (Week 13). You need 8 entries. Count yours now. If you're short, this week's collaborative work, the micro-defence experience, or the portfolio curation are all valid sources for entries. Last chance."

### 2:35--2:40 | Closing Announcements
**Format:** Whole class

"Next week is your last working week. You'll iterate on your collaborative artifact, finalize your Decision Logs, draft your Evidence-Indexed Reflection, and write your last Goal Document entry. Roles continue."

"Use the cross-group feedback you got today. If your reader couldn't follow your Goal Document progression, revise. That progression is the story you'll tell in your reflection."

### 2:40--2:45 | Closing
**Format:** Whole class

- "Three micro-defence rounds, done. Think about the difference between your first one in Week 4 and today. Can you explain your work more clearly? Can you use the right terms? That growth is real and it's visible."
- "Week 13: last working week. Collaborative artifact finalized. Decision Logs due. Evidence-Indexed Reflection drafted. Instructor reads your Goal Document one more time."
- "Week 14: final submissions and sharing."

## After Class

- **Micro-defence scoring:** Return written scores within one week. Compare each student's performance across all three micro-defences (Weeks 4, 8, 12) to assess progression. Note patterns: which checklist criteria improved, which are still weak.
- **Schedule remaining micro-defences** for the start of Week 13 if needed.
- **Review cross-group peer feedback.** Spot-check whether peer responses are specific and substantive. Flag any that are generic ("good job") for a brief comment to the peer about what useful feedback looks like.
- **Check collaborative project progress.** Review shared repositories for: Issues updated, PRs submitted, role-based activity visible. Flag groups where one member has contributed nothing.
- **Mid-semester artifact package feedback:** Must be returned by now (graded in Week 10, feedback due by Week 12). If outstanding, return immediately.

## Language Development Summary

- **Vocabulary:** No new terms. Full 40-term repertoire applied throughout. Key recycled terms during portfolio curation: all assessment-related terms (verification, troubleshoot, help request, log, review) and all Git terms (repository, commit, pull request, issue, push, restore).
- **Speaking:** Oral micro-defence (3-4 min sustained technical explanation, third iteration); peer feedback delivery; evidence-mapping discussion with partner; cross-group peer response discussion; collaborative progress report negotiation within group
- **Reading:** Own full portfolio across 12 weeks (Decision Logs, artifact packages, Goal Document, PRs, Issues); CLO descriptions; partner's full Goal Document (11 entries, cross-group); instructor's worked example of Evidence-Indexed Reflection; cross-group peer's written feedback
- **Writing:** In-class diagnostic (~50-100 words, unmediated, third iteration); peer response notes (cross-group, 2 written answers about partner's progression); collaborative progress report (group writing, evaluative); individual Goal Document entry (temporal comparison, subordination -- most linguistically demanding comparison yet); evidence-mapping notes (2-3 CLOs)
- **Listening:** Portfolio curation worked example; peer's micro-defence; instructor follow-up questions; cross-group partner's feedback discussion; group members' contributions to progress report

## Assessment Alignment

- **Oral Micro-Defence 3 (part of 25% component):**
  - In-class written diagnostic (10% of micro-defence grade): unmediated writing about a technical problem, third data point for progression
  - Oral walkthrough (30% fluency + 50% task = 80% of micro-defence grade): sustained explanation of work and learning
  - Peer feedback (10% of micro-defence grade): assessed on the quality of the *peer's* feedback, not the presenter's work
  - **CLOs 2 and 5 assessed** (third formal assessment; comparison with Weeks 4 and 8 shows progression)
- **Decision Logs:** Due next week (Week 13). Students should have 8 entries. Entries 6-8 are assessed summatively.
- **Goal Document:** Entry 12 (individual: temporal comparison with Week 4) + collaborative progress report. Not graded. Instructor reads at Week 13 (next reading point) and returns brief final comments on the full arc.
- **Evidence-Indexed Reflection:** Students began mapping evidence to CLOs this week. Due Week 14. Not graded this week.
- **Git Episodes:** Collaboration episode evidence may continue accumulating. Due Week 14.
- **Collaborative roles:** Roles continue. Coordinator updates Issues. Builder continues production. Reviewer checks quality. Week 13 is the last working week.
