# GEAP 103 Week 13 Lesson Plan: The Conversation

**Context:** 3 x 55 min (150 min instruction + 15-min break), B1 learners, BYOD, Office 365 + Copilot
**Week 13 of 14** | Phase 3: Creation

**Learning Objectives (Week 13):**
1. Demonstrate metacognitive reflection in a one-on-one oral conversation, making claims about CLOs, pointing to evidence, and evaluating evidence strength (all CLOs)
2. Complete and submit the collaborative artifact with contribution statements (CLO 7)
3. Write a final Goal Document entry tracing change across the full semester (evaluative, temporal comparison)
4. Prepare for Week 14 sharing event

**No new vocabulary.** Students should be using course terminology fluently.

**No session review this week.** The oral reflection IS the reflection activity.

**This is the centrepiece assessment of the course's metacognitive thread.** The oral reflection (10%) is the culminating unmediated spoken performance. Students have been practising explaining their work to AI every week since Week 1. By Week 13, explaining work orally is a well-practised skill, not a novel demand. The session review from Week 12 (defending a DL self-assessment to a sceptical instructor) was the direct rehearsal.

**Goal Thread -- Final Written Entry:**
Students read their entire Goal Document from Week 1 and write about how their goals changed and what they learned about what they want. This is the highest language demand of the semester: temporal comparison across 13 weeks, evaluative judgment, and honest self-assessment. The student who wrote "I wish my computer could..." in Week 1 now writes about what they can do and how their thinking changed.

## Segment A: Pre-Tagging + Oral Reflections Begin (0:00--0:40)

### 0:00--0:05 | Framing
**Format:** Whole class
**Language skills:** Listening

"Today I talk to each of you one-on-one for 4 minutes about your learning. This is the oral reflection — 10% of your grade. While you wait, you'll finalize your collaborative artifact and write your last Goal Document entry."

"The conversation goes like this: I ask you to pick one CLO you feel strongest about. You show me the evidence. You tell me how strong it is. Then I pick a second CLO and you do the same. Four minutes total."

### 0:05--0:10 | Pre-Tagging
**Format:** Individual (whole class, simultaneous)
**Language skills:** Reading (own portfolio, evidence map from Week 12)

"Open your portfolio. You have 5 minutes to tag your evidence. For 3-4 CLOs, open or bookmark the specific artifact you would point to. Use your evidence map from last week."

Students tag their portfolio: open Decision Log entries, artifact packages, Git history, PRs, or Goal Document entries that serve as evidence for specific CLOs. This reduces navigation friction during the conversation.

Display:

> **Pre-tagging checklist:**
> - [ ] Open your evidence map from Week 12
> - [ ] For 3-4 CLOs: bookmark the artifact you'd point to
> - [ ] Know which CLO is your strongest
> - [ ] Know which CLO you're weakest on (I might ask about it)

### 0:10--0:40 | Oral Reflections (First Block) + Structured Work
**Format:** One-on-one conversations + individual/group work for the rest of the class
**Language skills:** Speaking (metacognitive claims, evidence evaluation), listening (instructor questions)

**Oral reflection logistics:**

At 5 min per student (4 min conversation + 1 min transition):

| Class size | Reflection time needed | Remaining for structured work |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| 15 | 75 min | 70 min |
| 20 | 100 min | 45 min |
| 24 | 120 min | 25 min |

Reflections run through Segments A, B, and C. The instructor conducts reflections at a designated station (a desk, a corner) while the rest of the class works on the structured tasks below.

**Reflection process (per student):**
1. Instructor asks: "Pick one CLO you feel strongest about. Show me the evidence."
2. Student makes a claim ("I can now..."), points to a pre-tagged artifact, evaluates the evidence ("This is strong because..." or "This is OK but I wish I had...")
3. Instructor asks one follow-up question
4. Instructor selects a second CLO (typically one the student might be weaker on)
5. Student responds. Hard stop at 4 minutes — use a visible timer.

**This is unmediated.** No AI assistance. The student's portfolio is open for reference, but the language is entirely their own.

**Scoring (during or immediately after each conversation):**

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Excellent | Specific claims about 2 CLOs; points to appropriate evidence; honest evaluation of evidence strength; genuine growth areas; can answer follow-ups |
| Proficient | Claims present for 2 CLOs; evidence identified; some evaluation of quality; adequate follow-up answers |
| Developing | General claims; vague evidence links; no evaluation of quality; struggles with follow-ups |
| Insufficient | Cannot articulate claims; no evidence identified; cannot engage |

If a student freezes: "Look at your Decision Logs. Which entry are you most proud of? Tell me about it."

**Structured work while waiting (displayed):**

> While you wait for your reflection, work through this finalization checklist:
>
> 1. **Collaborative artifact:** Run Accessibility Checker on the final version. Fix any issues.
> 2. **Process documentation:** Complete your process documentation for the collaborative project (what AI did, what you changed, what you checked). In your own words.
> 3. **Peer response notes:** Have you incorporated feedback from the cross-group review?
> 4. **Sharing preparation:** Prepare for Week 14. What will you show? What will you say? Write a 1-minute explanation of one design decision you made.

This checklist is a deliverable: students submit it (checked off, with notes) by end of session. It is ungraded but provides structure and accountability.

## Segment B: Reflections Continue + Collaborative Artifact Finalization (0:40--1:35)

### 0:40--1:25 | Oral Reflections Continue + Collaborative Work
**Format:** Same as Segment A — instructor conducts reflections; students work

Students continue through the finalization checklist and collaborative artifact work:
- **Coordinator:** Checks all Issues are resolved or closed. Writes a brief project summary.
- **Builder:** Completes any remaining production. Ensures the artifact is functional.
- **Reviewer:** Final review pass. Runs Accessibility Checker. Writes or updates verification notes.

**Collaborative artifact submission at 1:25:** Groups submit their collaborative artifact (commit to shared repository or submit through established process).

**Contribution statements** (each student writes individually, 2-3 sentences):
- What they built or produced
- What PRs they submitted and reviewed
- What they contributed to the group's planning and quality

"This is checked against your Git history. Be accurate."

### 1:25--1:35 | Commit + Submit Collaborative Artifact
**Format:** Group + individual
**Language skills:** Writing (commit messages, contribution statements)

Groups commit and submit. Each member submits their individual contribution statement alongside the group submission.

## Segment C: Reflections Continue + Final Goal Document (1:50--2:45)

### 1:50--2:15 | Oral Reflections Continue (if needed) + Final Goal Document
**Format:** Individual reading and writing (while reflections continue)
**Language skills:** Reading (own Goal Document, all entries), writing (evaluative, temporal comparison, subordination)

For students not currently in a reflection:

"This is your last Goal Document entry. Go back to the beginning. Read your Week 1 entry. Read every entry after that. Notice how your language changed. Notice how your thinking changed. Then write about what you see."

**Step 1 -- Read** (10 min): Students read their entire Goal Document from Week 1 to Week 12. No writing yet.

**Step 2 -- Write** (15 min):

**Prompt** (displayed): "Read your Goal Document from the beginning. Write a final entry that answers: What did I learn about what I want? How did my goals change? What can I do now that I couldn't do in Week 1?"

**Sentence frames** *(optional — use if you're stuck)*:
- "When I started, I thought _____. Now I think _____."
- "The most important thing I learned is _____ because _____."
- "My goals changed from _____ to _____ because _____."
- "I can now _____, which I couldn't do before because _____."

**Instructor note:** This is the culminating language moment of the Goal Document arc. The transformation in articulation from Week 1 to now IS the course.

### 2:15--2:20 | Commit Final Goal Document
**Format:** Individual
**Language skills:** Writing (commit messages)

"Final Goal Document entry committed. 13 entries across 13 weeks."

### 2:20--2:35 | Week 14 Preparation
**Format:** Individual, then pairs
**Language skills:** Speaking (rehearsing presentation), listening (partner's rehearsal)

"Next week is the last class. You'll share what you built — individually and as a group. Let's prepare."

**Individual** (7 min): Plan your informal presentation:
- What will you show? (Your artifact, your portfolio, your favourite DL entry)
- What will you say about what you learned?
- Is this what you set out to make? What changed?

**Pair rehearsal with A/B turns** (8 min): Partner A presents for 2 min, Partner B asks one question. Switch. "Did your partner's explanation make sense? What was the strongest moment?"

**Group check** (if time): Groups briefly confirm who introduces (Coordinator), who demonstrates (Builder), who evaluates (Reviewer) for the Week 14 group presentation.

### 2:35--2:45 | Closing
**Format:** Whole class

- "Today I heard each of you talk about your learning. That conversation — making claims, pointing to evidence, evaluating honestly — is a skill that transfers to every course and every job."
- "Your collaborative artifact is submitted. Your Goal Document has 13 entries. Next week: final submissions and sharing."
- "Next week you submit: (1) Final Artifact Package (product + documentation + verification + sharing evidence), (2) Evidence-Indexed Reflection is done (that was today's conversation). Come ready to show what you built and say what you learned."
- "I'll read your Goal Documents this week — all 13 entries — and return brief final comments."

## After Class

- **Score oral reflections (10%).** Use the rubric above. Score during or immediately after each conversation.
- **Read all Goal Documents (entries 1-13).** Return brief final comments by Week 14. Comment on the arc: how thinking and language developed.
- **Check contribution statements against Git history.** Flag mismatches for conversation before grading.
- **Review collaborative artifacts.** Groups that didn't finish: brief extension to start of Week 14.
- **Spot-check finalization checklists.** Students should have them completed.
- **Begin grading DLs** (submitted Week 11) if not already done. Need to be complete before final grade calculation.

## Language Development Summary

- **Vocabulary:** No new terms. All 40 terms deployed naturally in oral reflection, contribution statements, and Goal Document writing. The oral reflection is the best single indicator of whether vocabulary has been acquired to productive level.
- **Speaking:** Oral reflection (4 min one-on-one: making claims, pointing to evidence, evaluating strength, answering follow-ups — the culminating unmediated spoken performance); pair rehearsal for Week 14 presentations
- **Reading:** Own entire Goal Document (13 entries — the most sustained reading of the semester); own portfolio (pre-tagging); finalization checklist; contribution statement guidance
- **Writing:** Final Goal Document entry 13 (temporal comparison across 13 weeks, subordination, evaluative judgment — the most linguistically demanding written entry); contribution statement (descriptive, accountable); finalization checklist notes; commit messages
- **Listening:** Instructor's oral reflection framing; partner's rehearsal presentation; closing announcements

## Assessment Alignment

- **Oral Evidence-Indexed Reflection (10% of final grade):** One-on-one, 4 min per student. 2 CLOs discussed. Scored on: specificity of claims, appropriateness of evidence, honesty of evaluation, ability to answer follow-ups. (All CLOs)
- **Collaborative artifact submitted:** With contribution statements. Not graded separately but verified against Git history for CLO 7 evidence.
- **Goal Document:** Final written entry (entry 13). Not graded. Instructor reads all 13 entries and returns brief final comments.
- **Finalization checklist:** Ungraded. Provides structure during reflections.
- **Week 14 preview:** Students prepare for sharing event and final artifact package submission.
