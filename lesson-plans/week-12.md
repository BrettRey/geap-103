# GEAP 103 Week 12 Lesson Plan: Is It Working?

**Context:** 3 x 55 min (150 min instruction + 15-min break), B1 learners, BYOD, Office 365 + Copilot
**Week 12 of 14** | Phase 3: Creation

**Learning Objectives (Week 12):**
1. Submit Git Episodes (3 required: Restore, Error Recovery, Collaboration) (CLOs 1, 7)
2. Map portfolio evidence to CLOs and evaluate evidence strength (preparation for Week 13 oral reflection)
3. Continue collaborative building with rotated roles (CLO 7)
4. Write a Goal Document entry comparing Week 4 with now (temporal comparison, subordination)

**No new vocabulary.** Consolidation of all 40 terms from Weeks 1-9.

**Session review this week:** "AI plays an instructor asking about one of your Decision Log entries. Defend your self-assessment rating." AI role: Sceptical instructor. Communicative demand: Justifying a self-evaluation with evidence.

**Goal Thread -- Progress Report:**
Students compare where they are now with where they were in Week 4. This requires temporal comparison ("In Week 4, I planned to... Now I am..."), subordination ("Although I expected..."), and evaluative language ("The biggest change is... because..."). This is the most linguistically demanding comparison the Goal Document has asked for.

## Segment A: Portfolio Curation + Git Episodes (0:00--0:40)

### 0:00--0:10 | Git Episodes Submission + Check
**Format:** Individual, then whole class
**Language skills:** Reading (own evidence), writing (episode documentation)

"Git Episodes are due today. Three episodes: Restore, Error Recovery, Collaboration. Each needs the story (what happened) and the evidence (screenshot or log)."

Students open their Git Episodes evidence. Quick self-check:

> Count your episodes. Do you have:
> - [ ] Restore: recovered an earlier version (screenshot/log)
> - [ ] Error Recovery: something broke, you diagnosed it, you fixed it (error + fix)
> - [ ] Collaboration: a PR with description + one comment exchange (screenshot/link)
>
> If you're missing one, you have until end of class. If you're missing two, come see me.

Students who are complete: submit now. Students missing episodes: use collaborative building time to generate evidence (especially Collaboration — today's PR work counts).

### 0:10--0:30 | Worked Example: Mapping Evidence to CLOs
**Format:** Instructor demonstration
**Language skills:** Listening, reading (CLO descriptions, sample evidence)

"Next week is the oral reflection. You'll have a 4-minute conversation with me about your learning. I'll ask about 2 CLOs. You point to evidence and tell me how strong it is. Today you prepare."

Display the 7 CLOs:

| CLO | What it says (simplified) |
|-----|--------------------------|
| 1 | Manage files and versions |
| 2 | Troubleshoot |
| 3 | Guide AI tools |
| 4 | Evaluate AI text |
| 5 | Communicate |
| 6 | Produce digital products |
| 7 | Collaborate |

Instructor models the process for one CLO:

> **CLO 4: Evaluate AI text**
> *Claim:* "I can identify when AI output is wrong and explain why."
> *Evidence:* Decision Log entry 5 — I rejected Copilot's chart because it used the wrong data range. I noticed because the totals didn't match my manual calculation.
> *Strength:* "This is strong evidence because it shows I checked independently, not just accepted it."
> *Growth area:* "I'm better at catching number errors than text errors."

"That's what the conversation sounds like. You make a claim, point to evidence, and evaluate how strong it is. Honest is better than impressive."

Then model a WEAK example:

> **CLO 1: Manage files and versions**
> *Claim:* "I can manage files."
> *Evidence:* "I have a folder."
> *Strength:* "It's good."

"What's wrong with this? The claim is vague, the evidence is thin, and the evaluation says nothing. Compare that with: 'My Week 9 commit history shows 7 weeks of consistent descriptive file names and folder paths. That's stronger evidence than the Week 4 folder screenshot, which just shows I organized once.'"

### 0:30--0:40 | Students Map Evidence for 3-4 CLOs
**Format:** Individual work with structured A/B pair check
**Language skills:** Reading (own portfolio), writing (evidence mapping notes)

"The evidence map shows what's actually in your portfolio -- not what you remember doing, but what you can point to right now. If you can't find evidence for a CLO, that tells you something important. Don't invent evidence. Name the gap."

Students create an evidence map:

| CLO | Best evidence | Where | Strong or weak? |
|-----|--------------|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | ... | ... | ... |

**Writing** (7 min): Map at least 3-4 CLOs.

**Pair check with A/B turns** (3 min): "Partner A: tell me your strongest CLO and your evidence. Partner B: is that convincing? Then switch."

## Segment B: Collaborative Building + Roles (0:40--1:35)

### 0:40--0:45 | Role Rotation + Planning
**Format:** Small groups
**Language skills:** Speaking (planning, negotiation)

"Roles rotate this week. Last week's Coordinator becomes this week's Builder (or Reviewer). Everyone gets a different perspective."

Groups confirm new roles and plan the session:
- **Coordinator:** What Issues are open? What's the priority for today?
- **Builder:** What artifact work remains? What's the most important thing to build?
- **Reviewer:** What hasn't been reviewed yet? What quality checks are needed?

### 0:45--1:25 | Collaborative Building
**Format:** Small groups with instructor circulation
**Language skills:** Writing (PRs, comments, Issues), speaking (coordination), reading (AI output, partner's work)

Groups build. Same role-based structure as Week 11 but with rotated roles.

**AI reflection pause at 1:05** (2 min): "Stop building. Write one sentence: what did you just ask AI to do, and what did you decide about the result?"

**Git Episodes — last chance:** Students missing the Collaboration episode: submit a PR with description + comment exchange during this building time. Remind: "If you need a Collaboration episode, do it now. Submit a PR, get a comment from your partner, save the screenshot."

Instructor circulates:
- **Git Episodes:** "Have you submitted your three episodes? If not, which one are you missing?"
- **Role check:** "What's your new role? How is it different from last week?"
- **Progress:** "What has your group accomplished? What's the plan for finishing by next week?"

### 1:25--1:35 | Commit + Git Episodes Final Submission
**Format:** Individual
**Language skills:** Writing (commit messages, episode documentation)

All students commit their work. Students who haven't submitted Git Episodes: submit now. This is the deadline.

## Segment C: Goal Document + Session Review + Closing (1:50--2:45)

### 1:50--2:10 | Goal Document: Comparing Week 4 with Now
**Format:** Individual reading, then writing
**Language skills:** Reading (own Goal Document), writing (temporal comparison, subordination, evaluative)

"Go back to your Week 4 Goal Document entry — the one where you took stock after Phase 1. Read it. Then write about what changed."

**Reading** (5 min): Students read their Week 4 entry (and optionally skim Weeks 1-3).

**Prompt** (displayed): "Compare what you wrote in Week 4 with where you are now. What changed? Why?"

**Sentence frames** *(optional — use if you're stuck)*:
- "In Week 4, I planned to _____. Now I am _____."
- "The biggest change is _____ because _____."
- "I didn't expect _____, but it turned out that _____."
- "Although I thought _____, what actually happened is _____."

**Writing** (15 min): Students write entry 12 in their Goal Document. This requires temporal comparison: looking back 8 weeks and articulating what changed and why.

### 2:10--2:12 | Bridging Activity
**Format:** Individual, silent

"In two minutes, you'll defend one of your Decision Log self-assessments to AI. AI is playing a sceptical instructor. Think: which DL entry did you rate yourself highest on? What would you say if I asked 'Why did you rate that Excellent?'"

### 2:12--2:25 | Session Review (Speaking Practice)
**Format:** Individual, AI voice mode
**Language skills:** Speaking (justifying a self-evaluation, defending with evidence)

**Role-setting prompt** (on slide): "Speaking practice. I'm a B1 English student practising explaining my work. Ask me follow-up questions. If my explanation is vague, tell me what's unclear and ask me to be more specific. Don't do the explaining for me."

**This week's scenario** (on slide): "AI plays an instructor asking about one of your Decision Log entries. Defend your self-assessment rating. Why did you rate it the way you did?"

AI role: Sceptical instructor.

Students talk to AI for 10-12 minutes. The AI challenges their self-assessment: "You rated this Excellent. What makes it Excellent and not just Proficient?" "What evidence supports your rating?"

This directly rehearses the oral reflection (Week 13), where the instructor will ask similar questions.

**Self-check** (1 min): "Write two sentences: What rating did you defend? What was the hardest question AI asked?"

**Note:** Voice mode starts a new chat.

### 2:25--2:35 | Week 13 Preparation
**Format:** Whole class
**Language skills:** Listening, reading (oral reflection format)

"Next week is the oral reflection. Here's what happens."

Display:

> **Oral Reflection (Week 13)**
> - 4 minutes, one-on-one with the instructor
> - Your portfolio is open with evidence pre-tagged
> - I pick 2 CLOs to discuss (not all 7)
> - You make a claim ("I can now..."), point to evidence, and evaluate how strong it is
> - I ask one follow-up question per CLO
>
> **Before the conversation starts:** You'll have 5 minutes to tag your portfolio (bookmark the evidence you'd point to for 3-4 CLOs)
>
> **While you wait:** You'll work on a finalization checklist for your final artifact package (Week 14)

"The evidence mapping you did today is your preparation. Review it before next class. Know where your strongest evidence is for each CLO."

### 2:35--2:45 | Closing
**Format:** Whole class

- "Git Episodes are submitted. That's 10% done."
- "Your evidence map is your preparation for next week's oral reflection. Review it before class."
- "Collaborative work continues next week during the finalization time. Roles persist."
- "Next week: I talk to each of you one-on-one for 4 minutes about your learning. This is the culmination of 13 weeks of speaking practice. You've been doing this with AI every week. Now you do it with me."

## After Class

- **Grade Git Episodes (10%).** Checklist-based: 3 required episodes. Short oral confirmation for evidence that contains no errors, no personal voice markers, and looks templated (e.g., generic screenshot descriptions that don't match the student's oral level). A 2-minute conversation: "Can you tell me what happened in this episode in your own words?"
- **Review evidence maps** (spot-check). Are students finding appropriate evidence for CLOs? Flag students whose maps are empty or generic for a brief check-in before Week 13.
- **Review collaborative project progress.** Groups should be close to completion. Flag groups that need extra time.
- **Prepare Week 13 oral reflections.** Read each student's DL entries (submitted Week 11) and Goal Document. Identify 2 CLOs to ask about per student (one the student is likely strong on, one potentially weaker). Prepare follow-up questions.
- **Mid-semester artifact package feedback** must be returned by now.

## Language Development Summary

- **Vocabulary:** No new terms. All 40 terms deployed in evidence mapping, collaborative work, and session review. Key recycled terms: all assessment vocabulary (verify, evaluate, process, outcome) and all Git terms.
- **Speaking:** A/B pair check on evidence mapping; in-group role-based coordination; **session review** (12 min AI voice: defending DL self-assessment to a sceptical instructor — directly rehearses Week 13 oral reflection)
- **Reading:** Own portfolio (full curation across 12 weeks); CLO descriptions; evidence mapping examples (strong vs. weak); own Goal Document Week 4 entry; partner's evidence map
- **Writing:** Git Episodes documentation; evidence mapping notes (3-4 CLOs); Goal Document entry 12 (temporal comparison, subordination); AI reflection pause sentences; session review self-check; commit messages
- **Listening:** Evidence mapping worked example; partner's evidence claim; AI follow-up questions during session review; Week 13 oral reflection format explanation

## Assessment Alignment

- **Git Episodes due (10% of final grade):** 3 required episodes (Restore, Error Recovery, Collaboration). Checklist-based. (CLOs 1, 7)
- **Session review** (formative, not graded): Week 12 scenario directly rehearses the oral reflection — defending self-evaluations with evidence
- **Goal Document:** Entry 12 (temporal comparison with Week 4). Not graded. Instructor reads at Week 13 (final reading point).
- **Evidence mapping:** Formative preparation for Week 13 oral reflection. Not graded, but students without an evidence map will struggle in the reflection.
- **Collaborative work:** Continues. Roles rotated. Groups should be near completion.
- **Week 13 preview:** Oral reflection format explained. Students know what to prepare.
