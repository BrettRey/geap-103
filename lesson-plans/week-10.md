# GEAP 103 Week 10 Lesson Plan: Building My Thing (Part 2) + Submission

**Context:** 3 x 55 min (150 min instruction + 15-min break), B1 learners, BYOD, Office 365 + Copilot
**Week 10 of 14** | Phase 2: Building (final week)

**Learning Objectives (Week 10):**
1. Complete and submit a mid-semester artifact package (product + process documentation + sharing evidence) (CLO 6)
2. Write a verification note describing what was checked and how (CLOs 4, 5)
3. Compare the finished artifact with the original plan and write honestly about the gap (evaluative, subordinating language)
4. Begin collaborative project formation by identifying shared interests and complementary skills (CLO 7 begins in earnest)

**No new vocabulary.** Consolidation of all 40 terms from Weeks 1-9.

**Goal Thread -- Reflection on Gap:**
This is a major language moment. Students compare what they planned with what they actually built. The language requires subordination ("Although I planned to..., what I actually..."), concession ("Even though I wanted..., I ended up..."), and honest evaluation ("The biggest difference is... because..."). This is the transition from Phase 2 (planning and justifying) to Phase 3 (evaluating and comparing). The Goal Document complexity level shifts here: students are no longer just describing what they want -- they are assessing what they did.

## Segment A: Verification and Quality Check (0:00--0:40)

### 0:00--0:05 | Framing
**Format:** Whole class
**Language skills:** Listening

"Today you finish and submit your mid-semester artifact package. Remember: the package is three things -- what you built, documentation of how you built it in your own words, and evidence of sharing. But before you submit, you need to check your work. Not just 'does it look OK?' but 'did I build what I said I would?'"

### 0:05--0:25 | Worked Example: The Gap Between Plan and Result
**Format:** Instructor demonstration
**Language skills:** Listening, reading (Goal Document text, Accessibility Checker output)

Instructor shows a worked example of the full final review process using their own (or a sample) artifact:

**Step 1 -- Re-read the Goal Document.** Instructor displays a Goal Document entry from a few weeks ago (the project plan) and the finished artifact side by side. "Here's what I said I would build. Here's what I actually built. Let's compare."

**Step 2 -- Name the gap honestly.** Instructor thinks aloud: "I planned to include three charts, but I only finished two. The third one was too complicated -- I couldn't get Copilot to generate the right format, and I ran out of time. That's not a failure. That's useful information. The honest thing to write is: 'I planned three charts but completed two because the third required a chart type I couldn't figure out in the time available.'"

**Step 3 -- Run the Accessibility Checker.** Instructor runs the checker on the artifact. Fixes one issue on screen (e.g., missing alt text). "This is part of verification. You check that what you built is usable, not just finished."

**Step 4 -- Write the verification note.** Instructor writes a brief verification note on the projector:

> "I checked my spreadsheet with the Accessibility Checker. It found one image without alt text, which I added. I also tested the formulas by entering sample data and comparing the results with a calculator. The budget totals match. I re-read my Goal Document from Week 8 and confirmed that I built the budget tracker I described, except for the savings projection chart, which I did not complete."

"This is written in my own words. It says what I checked, what I found, and what I didn't finish. If this sounds very different from how I talk, that's a red flag."

### 0:25--0:40 | Artifact Package Requirements Review
**Format:** Instructor-led, Q&A
**Language skills:** Listening, reading (requirements)

Display the artifact package components:

| Component | What to submit | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| **Product** | The artifact itself (document, spreadsheet, presentation, game, etc.) | Must be functional. Accessibility features where applicable. |
| **Process documentation** | Written in your own words: what AI did, what you changed and why, what you checked | Not AI-generated prose. Should sound like you. |
| **Verification note** | What you checked, what you found, what you fixed, what you didn't finish | Honest. Includes the gap between plan and result. |
| **Evidence of sharing** | How you explained or showed your work to someone else | Week 9 peer check-in counts. Any other sharing you did. |

"Questions? This is due by the end of class today. If your artifact isn't perfect, that's fine. The documentation matters as much as the product. Tell me what you built, how you built it, and what you'd change."

## Segment B: Planning + Finalisation + Submission (0:40--1:35)

### 0:40--0:45 | Pre-Task Planning
**Format:** Individual reflection
**Language skills:** Reading (own Goal Document), writing (task list)

"Open your Goal Document. Re-read your project plan from Weeks 7-8 and your task list from Week 9. Check your artifact against your stated plan. What's done? What's not? What do you need to finish in the next 45 minutes?"

Students write a brief priority list (not a full Goal Document entry -- that comes in Segment C):
- What is complete
- What still needs to be done
- What they will skip if they run out of time

### 0:45--1:20 | Finalisation and Submission
**Format:** Individual work with instructor circulation
**Language skills:** Writing (process documentation, verification note), reading (AI output, Accessibility Checker results)

Students work on three tasks in whatever order makes sense for their situation:

1. **Finish the artifact.** Complete any remaining work on the product itself.
2. **Write process documentation.** In their own words: what AI did, what they changed, what they checked. Students who took notes during Week 9 build on those. Students who didn't: reconstruct from memory and their Decision Log entries.
3. **Write the verification note.** Run the Accessibility Checker (if applicable). Compare the artifact against the Goal Document plan. Write what was checked, what was found, and what was not completed.

Instructor circulates with two purposes:
1. **Submission support:** Help students compile their package (where to put files, how to submit).
2. **Process documentation check:** "Can you read me your process documentation? Does it sound like how you'd explain this to me in person?" If it sounds markedly unlike the student's spoken English, prompt them to rewrite in simpler language.

### 1:20--1:35 | Commit and Submit
**Format:** Individual
**Language skills:** Writing (commit messages)

Students commit their complete artifact package to their portfolio repository. Then submit through whatever submission process the instructor has established (OneDrive folder, LMS upload, or GitHub link).

Commit message should reflect what's in the package: "Submitted mid-semester artifact package: budget tracker + process documentation + verification note."

Students who finish early: begin reading their Goal Document from the beginning (preparation for the reflection in Segment C).

## Segment C: Reflection + Collaborative Project Formation (1:50--2:45)

### 1:50--2:10 | Goal Document: Reflection on the Gap
**Format:** Individual writing
**Language skills:** Writing (evaluative, subordinating, concessive)

"You've submitted your artifact. Now: the most important writing of the semester so far. Compare what you planned with what you built. This is hard because it requires a kind of honesty that's also a language skill. You need to write sentences like 'Although I planned to..., what I actually built is...' and 'The biggest difference is... because...'"

**Prompt** (displayed): "Compare what you planned with what you built. Write 3-4 sentences about the gap."

**Sentence frames** (displayed):
- "I set out to build _____. What I actually built is _____."
- "The biggest difference is _____ because _____."
- "If I had more time, I would change _____."
- "I learned that _____ is harder/easier than I expected because _____."

Students write (15 min). Some students will have built exactly what they planned. That's fine -- they can write about what went as expected and why. The language challenge is the same: evaluating a result against an intention.

**Instructor note:** This is a Phase 3 language moment (evaluating and comparing) even though Phase 3 officially starts next week. The complexity shift is real: students are moving from "I will..." to "Although I planned..., what I actually..." This requires subordination, hedging, and concession -- language features that many B1 learners are still developing. The sentence frames scaffold this.

### 2:10--2:35 | Collaborative Project Formation
**Format:** Whole class, then small groups
**Language skills:** Speaking (expressing preferences, negotiating), listening (partner's ideas)

"You've built something on your own. For the last four weeks of the course, you'll build something with a partner or a small group. This is not the same as your individual project. It's a new thing."

**Step 1 -- Interest inventory (5 min).** Each student writes on a card or shared document:
- "I'm interested in _____."
- "I'm good at _____."
- "I want to learn _____."

**Step 2 -- Gallery or round-robin (10 min).** Students circulate and read each other's cards. Or: quick round-robin where each student shares their three statements aloud (30 seconds each, ~10 min for 20 students).

**Step 3 -- Group formation (10 min).** Instructor facilitates pairing/grouping based on shared interests or complementary skills. Groups of 2-3 (groups of 4 only if needed). Students do not need to share the same project type; complementary skills are valuable ("I can do the data, you can do the writing").

Once groups form, they begin discussing: "What do *we* want to build together?" This is the beginning, not a commitment. Groups will define their project formally in Week 11.

**Instructor note:** Do not force consensus today. The goal is to form groups and start talking. Some groups will know immediately what they want to do. Others will need Week 11 to figure it out. Both are fine.

### 2:35--2:40 | DL Feedback Round 2 Returned
**Format:** Individual reading
**Language skills:** Reading (instructor feedback)

Instructor returns written feedback on Decision Log entries 3-5. Students read their feedback.

"Look at what I said about your Decision Logs. Compare it with the feedback I gave you on entries 1-2 in Week 6. Did you improve in the areas I flagged? Your remaining entries (6-8) are assessed summatively, so this is your last feedback before the final submission."

### 2:40--2:45 | Closing
**Format:** Whole class

- "You submitted your mid-semester artifact package today. That's a real thing you built, documented, and shared. Well done."
- "You also wrote about the gap between what you planned and what you built. That writing skill -- comparing intention with result -- is what the rest of the course is about."
- "Starting next week: Phase 3. You'll work with your group to build something together. The question shifts from 'What do I want?' to 'What do we want?' Same skills, bigger challenge."
- "Read your DL feedback this week. Think about what you want to improve in your remaining entries."

## After Class

- **Grade mid-semester artifact packages.** Use the Artifact Package rubric (Assessment Framework v4). Focus on: does the process documentation sound like the student? Is the verification note honest? Is there evidence of sharing? Return feedback by Week 12.
- **Note students who did not submit** or submitted incomplete packages. Follow up at the start of Week 11.
- **Review collaborative group formations.** Identify any students who were absent or didn't find a group. Plan to integrate them in Week 11.
- **Decision Log feedback round 2:** Should have been returned today. If not, return by start of Week 11 at the latest.

## Language Development Summary

- **Vocabulary:** No new terms. Consolidation of all 40 terms from Weeks 1-9 as students use relevant vocabulary in their artifact work, process documentation, and verification notes.
- **Speaking:** Brief priority-list sharing; collaborative project formation (expressing interests, negotiating with potential partners); informal sharing of completed artifacts
- **Reading:** Own Goal Document (full re-read for reflection); instructor's verification note example; Accessibility Checker output; DL feedback round 2; partner's interest inventory cards
- **Writing:** Verification note (descriptive, evaluative -- new genre this week); process documentation (own words, describing AI collaboration); Goal Document reflection (subordination, concession, evaluation -- the most linguistically demanding GD entry so far); interest inventory (brief self-description); commit messages
- **Listening:** Worked example of gap analysis and verification; partner's interests during group formation; instructor feedback discussion

## Assessment Alignment

- **Mid-Semester Artifact Package due (8% of final grade):** Product + process documentation + verification note + evidence of sharing (CLOs 4, 5, 6)
- **Decision Log feedback round 2 returned:** Written feedback on entries 3-5. Focus on whether Round 1 feedback was incorporated. Entries 6-8 are assessed summatively.
- **Goal Document:** Reflection on gap (Week 10 entry). Not graded. Instructor reads at Week 13 (next reading point).
- **CLO 7** (Collaborate): Collaborative project formation begins. Groups formed. No assessment yet, but the collaborative artifact and Git Episodes (collaboration episode) will be assessed in Weeks 11-14.
- **Phase transition:** This is the last week of Phase 2 (Building). Next week begins Phase 3 (Creation). The Goal Document complexity level shifts from "Planning and justifying" to "Evaluating and comparing."
