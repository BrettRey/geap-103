# GEAP 103 Week 9 Lesson Plan: Building My Thing (Part 1)

**Context:** 3 x 55 min (150 min instruction + 15-min break), B1 learners, BYOD, Office 365 + Copilot
**Week 9 of 14** | Phase 2: Building

**Learning Objectives (Week 9):**
1. Apply relevant tool skills to begin building a personal artifact that addresses a genuine need (CLO 6)
2. Write a concrete, specific task list that directs your own work session (CLO 3)
3. Evaluate whether early work matches the intention stated in the Goal Document (CLO 4)
4. Give structured peer feedback on alignment between a partner's stated goal and their artifact-in-progress

**New vocabulary (5 AWL terms):** design, draft, evaluate, requirement, focus

*Slide, layout, speaker notes, caption, and colour contrast are introduced contextually during the project-type demonstrations for students whose projects involve presentations. They are genre-specific terms, not AWL vocabulary.*

**Goal Thread -- Execution:**
The Goal Document becomes the work plan. Students have spent 8 weeks developing the language to say what they want. Now they use it to direct their own work. The sentence frames shift to imperative/planning language: "Today I will _____. I need to finish _____ before I can _____." This is the moment where language becomes the control layer in practice, not just in principle.

## Segment A: Vocabulary + Self-Selected Demonstrations (0:00--0:40)

### 0:00--0:12 | Vocabulary Pre-Training
**Format:** Instructor-led, then pair task
**Language skills:** Academic vocabulary building

**5 AWL terms:** design, draft, evaluate, requirement, focus

1. **Design:** "The intentional plan for how your artifact will work and look. Everyone is designing something this semester. 'I designed it this way because...' is the language of explaining your choices."

2. **Draft:** "A preliminary version. What you build today is a first draft. A draft is meant to be changed. 'This is my first draft' is not an apology -- it's a normal step in the process."

3. **Evaluate:** "To judge the quality of something against criteria. When your partner looks at your work and asks 'does this match your plan?', they are evaluating. You've been evaluating AI output since Week 2. Now you evaluate your own work."

4. **Requirement:** "Something the project must do or include. 'My requirement is that a screen reader can navigate it.' Requirements come from your Goal Document -- they are what you said you wanted."

5. **Focus:** "To concentrate attention on one specific thing. 'Today I will focus on the layout' means you're not doing everything -- you're choosing one task. Focus is how you turn a big plan into a work session."

**Pair task** (3 min): Display a vague project description:

> "I'm going to work on my project today."

Pairs rewrite it using at least 3 of the 5 terms to make it specific. Share 1-2 examples.

### 0:12--0:35 | Self-Selected Project-Type Demonstrations
**Format:** Small groups (self-selected by project type), then gallery walk
**Language skills:** Listening (worked example), reading (other groups' summaries), writing (1-sentence summary)

"You've been refining your ideas for 8 weeks. Today you start building. First, a short demonstration relevant to what you're actually making."

Group students by project type. Groupings are flexible based on what students are building (combine small groups as needed):

- **Document / novel group:** Pulling together structured or narrative content with Copilot + Accessibility Checker. Worked example: turning a rough outline into a structured, accessible document.
- **Data tool group:** Going further with Excel, including multi-sheet workbooks and charting. Worked example: linking summary data across sheets and creating a chart.
- **Presentation / video group:** Copilot in PowerPoint + speaker notes; Clipchamp basics. Worked example: generating slides from an outline, writing speaker notes, adding captions. *(Presentation-specific terms -- slide, layout, speaker notes, caption, colour contrast -- introduced here.)*
- **Game / app group:** Using AI to generate and test interactive content in a browser. Worked example: describing a simple quiz to AI and testing the output.
- **Art group:** AI image generation + design layout. Worked example: generating images with a specific prompt and arranging them.
- **Combination group:** Linking outputs across tools. Worked example: embedding a chart in a report.

Each demonstration: ~10 min. **Logistics for one instructor:** Run the 2 largest groups as sequential live demos (~10 min each). Other groups follow prepared step-by-step guides at their own pace (distributed via OneDrive). Instructor floats to guide groups between live demos.

**At least one demo should model AI failure:** e.g., in the document group, AI applies heading styles incorrectly or generates an inaccessible layout. The instructor diagnoses and revises. This prepares students for the gaps they'll encounter during building.

### 0:35--0:40 | Gallery Walk
**Format:** Whole class, mobile
**Language skills:** Writing (concise summary), reading (other groups' work)

Each group writes a 1-sentence summary on a shared slide or sticky note: "The most useful thing we learned is _____."

Students circulate and read all groups' summaries (3-5 min). This gives everyone exposure to techniques relevant to other project types.

## Segment B: Planning + Project Work (0:40--1:35)

### 0:40--0:50 | Pre-Task Planning
**Format:** Individual writing, then brief pair check with A/B turns
**Language skills:** Writing (planning language), reading (own Goal Document)

"Open your Goal Document. Re-read what you wrote in Weeks 7 and 8 -- your project plan, your risk assessment. Now write a concrete task list for today. Not 'work on my project.' Specific tasks with specific requirements."

Students write a task list directly in their Goal Document (this IS the Week 9 entry):

**Prompt** (displayed): "Write your task list for today. What specifically will you build in the next hour? What are the requirements?"

**Sentence frames** *(optional -- use if you're stuck)*:
- "Today I will focus on _____. The requirement is _____."
- "The first thing I need to do is _____ because _____."
- "My draft should include _____ by the end of today."
- "If I finish _____, I will move on to _____."

After writing (5 min): **Partner A reads their task list aloud (1.5 min), Partner B asks one clarifying question. Switch.** "Does your partner's list make sense? Is anything missing?"

### 0:50--1:35 | Project Work
**Format:** Individual work with instructor circulation
**Language skills:** Writing (artifact production, process documentation), reading (AI output, system feedback)

Students build. This is not free time. Students should be working toward specific tasks they defined in their task list.

**Process documentation mini-template** (displayed or in Goal Document): Students add a simple table to their Goal Document for tracking:

| AI prompt | AI output | My decision | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----|

"Every time you ask AI to do something, add a row. This takes 30 seconds and saves you from trying to remember next week."

Instructor circulates with three purposes:
1. **Progress check:** "What are you working on? Show me your task list. Which task are you on?"
2. **Technical support:** Help students who are stuck. Redirect to AI first: "Have you asked Copilot? What did it say?"
3. **Documentation check:** "Have you added a row to your process table? Show me."

**Hard documentation pause at 1:15** (25 min into building):

> "Stop building for 2 minutes. Open your process table. Write at least 3 rows: what did AI do, what did you decide, why? If you haven't been tracking, reconstruct from memory now. This documentation is required for the artifact package next week."

Then students resume building until 1:35.

**Decision Log entry (optional this week):** Students who want to write a DL entry should document a significant AI interaction during project work. Remind: 8 entries total across Weeks 2-11; this is one of the eligible weeks.

## Segment C: Peer Check-In + Session Review (1:50--2:45)

### 1:50--2:08 | Peer Check-In
**Format:** Pair work with structured A/B turn-taking
**Language skills:** Speaking (describing work-in-progress, evaluating alignment), listening (partner's description), reading (partner's Goal Document and artifact)

"Find your partner. Show each other what you've been building. The question: does this match what you described in your Goal Document?"

**Setup:** Both partners open their Goal Document (Weeks 7-8 entries) alongside their artifact, ideally side by side (split screen or two tabs). This reduces the split-attention cost of switching between documents.

**Partner A goes first** (8 min):
1. **Show:** Student A shows their artifact-in-progress (3 min)
2. **Compare:** Partner B reads A's Goal Document entries while looking at the artifact. "Does this match the plan? What's different?" (3 min)
3. **Feedback:** Partner B says one thing that matches and one thing that seems missing (2 min)

**Partner B goes next** (8 min): Same process, reversed.

**Guidance on screen:**
> When you evaluate your partner's work, ask:
> - Does this look like what they described in their Goal Document?
> - What's here that they planned?
> - What's missing?
> - What's here that they didn't plan?

### 2:08--2:20 | Decision Log / Continued Work + Commit
**Format:** Individual
**Language skills:** Writing (evaluative, technical), writing (commit messages)

Students who are writing a Decision Log entry this week: complete it now using the standard template.

Students who are not: continue project work or refine process documentation notes.

At 2:18: "Commit your work now. Descriptive message: what did you accomplish today? Not 'Updated files.'"

### 2:20--2:22 | Bridging Activity
**Format:** Individual, silent

"In two minutes, you'll explain something to AI. Think about this: you built something today, and it probably doesn't perfectly match what you intended. What's the gap? What did you want, and what did you get?"

### 2:22--2:35 | Session Review (Speaking Practice)
**Format:** Individual, AI voice mode
**Language skills:** Speaking (diagnosing a gap between intention and result, responding to follow-ups)

**Role-setting prompt** (on slide): "Speaking practice. I'm a B1 English student practising explaining my work. Ask me follow-up questions. If my explanation is vague, tell me what's unclear and ask me to be more specific. Don't do the explaining for me."

**This week's scenario** (on slide): "You built something but it doesn't do what you intended. Explain to AI what you wanted, what you got, and what you think went wrong."

AI role: Debugging partner.

Students talk to AI for 10-12 minutes, describing the gap between their Goal Document plan and their artifact draft. The AI pushes back when the description is vague: "What specifically doesn't match? What did you expect to see?"

Instructor circulates and listens. No intervention unless a student is stuck or silent.

**Self-check** (1 min): "Write two sentences: What was the gap between your plan and your draft? What did AI help you see?"

*If students are visibly exhausted at this point in the session, the instructor can simplify: "Tell AI one thing you're proud of from today's building." This still produces speaking practice, just at a lower cognitive demand.*

**Note:** Voice mode starts a new chat. The prompt is said aloud or pasted at the start.

### 2:35--2:45 | Closing
**Format:** Whole class

- "You started building. Look at your task list. What did you finish? What didn't you get to? That gap is useful information for next week."
- "Next week: you finish and submit your mid-semester artifact package. That means: the artifact itself, your process documentation (in your own words), and evidence of sharing. The peer check-in today counts as sharing."
- "Your artifact package includes three things: what you built, documentation of how you built it, and evidence that you explained it to someone."
- Remind: "If you haven't committed your work, do it now. Descriptive message."

## After Class

- **Spot-check task lists and process documentation** (not all students). Look for: specificity of task lists, evidence of process documentation during building, whether students are on track for the artifact package next week.

- **Session review patterns:** Were students able to articulate the gap between intention and result? This skill is central to the mid-semester artifact package (Week 10).

## Language Development Summary

- **Vocabulary:** 5 AWL terms (design, draft, evaluate, requirement, focus) pre-trained with vague-description rewriting task. Presentation-specific terms (slide, layout, speaker notes, caption, colour contrast) introduced contextually during project-type demos for relevant students.
- **Speaking:** Brief pair task list sharing (A/B turns); sustained peer check-in (describing work-in-progress, evaluating alignment, giving structured feedback with A/B turns); gallery walk discussion; **session review** (12 min AI voice: diagnosing gap between intention and result -- the first scenario focused on self-evaluation of one's own artifact)
- **Reading:** Partner's Goal Document (Weeks 7-8 entries); AI output during project work; other groups' gallery walk summaries; system feedback during building
- **Writing:** Task list as Goal Document entry 9 (planning/imperative language); process documentation notes during building; Decision Log entry (optional, evaluative); commit messages; gallery walk summary sentence; session review self-check sentence
- **Listening:** Project-type demonstration; partner's description of artifact-in-progress; partner's alignment feedback; AI follow-up questions during session review
