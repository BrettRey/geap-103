# GEAP 103 Week 9 Lesson Plan: Building My Thing (Part 1)

**Context:** 3 x 55 min (150 min instruction + 15-min break), B1 learners, BYOD, Office 365 + Copilot
**Week 9 of 14** | Phase 2: Building

**Learning Objectives (Week 9):**
1. Apply relevant tool skills to begin building a personal artifact that addresses a genuine need (CLO 6)
2. Write a concrete, specific task list that directs your own work session (CLO 3)
3. Evaluate whether early work matches the intention stated in the Goal Document (CLO 4)
4. Give structured peer feedback on alignment between a partner's stated goal and their artifact-in-progress

**New vocabulary (5):** slide, layout, speaker notes, caption, colour contrast
*(Introduced because some students' projects involve presentations. Others may not need these terms this week, but all benefit from the vocabulary for collaborative work later.)*

**Goal Thread -- Execution:**
The Goal Document becomes the work plan. Students have spent 8 weeks developing the language to say what they want. Now they use it to direct their own work. The sentence frames shift to imperative/planning language: "Today I will _____. I need to finish _____ before I can _____." This is the moment where language becomes the control layer in practice, not just in principle.

## Segment A: Vocabulary + Self-Selected Demonstrations (0:00--0:40)

### 0:00--0:10 | Vocabulary Pre-Training
**Format:** Whole class, instructor-led
**Language skills:** Listening, vocabulary building

"Before you start building, five terms that some of you will need today and all of you will need later."

| Term | Definition | Visual/example |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| **slide** | One page of a presentation | Show a single slide in PowerPoint |
| **layout** | The arrangement of text, images, and space on a page or slide | Show two layouts for the same content: one cluttered, one clear |
| **speaker notes** | Text below the slide that only the presenter sees | Show the notes pane in PowerPoint |
| **caption** | Text that describes an image or video for people who can't see it | Show a captioned image |
| **colour contrast** | The difference between text colour and background colour; low contrast is hard to read | Show a low-contrast slide (light grey on white) next to a high-contrast one |

For each term: display, say aloud, ask a volunteer to use it in a sentence. Connect to accessibility vocabulary from Week 5: "Colour contrast is related to the contrast and alt text you learned in Week 5. Captions are like alt text for video."

### 0:10--0:35 | Self-Selected Project-Type Demonstrations
**Format:** Small groups (self-selected by project type), then gallery walk
**Language skills:** Listening (worked example), reading (other groups' summaries), writing (1-sentence summary)

"You've been refining your ideas for 8 weeks. Today you start building. First, a short demonstration relevant to what you're actually making."

Instructor groups students by project type. Groupings are flexible based on what students are building (combine small groups as needed):

- **Document / novel group:** Pulling together structured or narrative content with Copilot + Accessibility Checker. Worked example: turning a rough outline into a structured, accessible document.
- **Data tool group:** Going further with Excel, including multi-sheet workbooks and charting. Worked example: linking summary data across sheets and creating a chart.
- **Presentation / video group:** Copilot in PowerPoint + speaker notes; Clipchamp basics. Worked example: generating slides from an outline, writing speaker notes, adding captions.
- **Game group:** Using AI to generate and test interactive content in a browser. Worked example: describing a simple quiz to AI and testing the output.
- **Art group:** AI image generation + design layout. Worked example: generating images with a specific prompt and arranging them in a document or slide.
- **Combination group:** Linking outputs across tools (e.g., data visualization embedded in a report). Worked example: embedding an Excel chart in a Word document.

Each demonstration: ~10 min. Instructor can run 2-3 demonstrations simultaneously (one live, others as prepared screencasts or step-by-step guides that students follow). Students in each group watch and ask questions.

### 0:35--0:40 | Gallery Walk
**Format:** Whole class, mobile
**Language skills:** Writing (concise summary), reading (other groups' work)

Each group writes a 1-sentence summary on a shared slide, whiteboard section, or sticky note: "The most useful thing we learned is _____."

Students circulate and read all groups' summaries (3-5 min). This gives everyone exposure to techniques relevant to other project types, which is useful for the collaborative project starting in Week 10.

## Segment B: Planning + Project Work (0:40--1:35)

### 0:40--0:50 | Pre-Task Planning
**Format:** Individual reflection, then brief pair check
**Language skills:** Writing (planning language), reading (own Goal Document)

"Open your Goal Document. Re-read what you wrote in Weeks 7 and 8 -- your project plan, your risk assessment. Now write a concrete task list for today. Not 'work on my project.' Specific tasks."

Students write a task list directly in their Goal Document (this IS the Week 9 Goal Document entry):

**Prompt** (displayed): "Write your task list for today. What specifically will you build in the next hour?"

**Sentence frames** (displayed):
- "Today I will _____. I need to finish _____ before I can _____."
- "The first thing I need to do is _____ because _____."
- "If I finish _____, I will move on to _____."

After writing (5 min): pairs briefly share task lists (2 min). "Does your partner's list make sense? Is anything missing?"

### 0:50--1:35 | Project Work
**Format:** Individual work with instructor circulation
**Language skills:** Writing (artifact production, process documentation), reading (AI output, system feedback)

Students build. This is not free time. Students should be working toward specific tasks they defined in their task list.

Instructor circulates with three purposes:
1. **Progress check:** "What are you working on right now? Show me your task list. Which task are you on?"
2. **Technical support:** Help students who are stuck. Redirect to AI first: "Have you asked Copilot? What did it say?"
3. **Language prompt:** "When you finish this task, write a sentence in your process notes about what AI did and what you changed."

Students should be documenting their process as they work -- brief notes about what AI did, what they changed, and what they checked. This documentation will become part of the artifact package submitted in Week 10.

**Decision Log entry (optional this week):** Students who want to write a Decision Log entry this week should document a significant AI interaction during their project work. Remind students that they need 8 entries total across 12 eligible weeks; this is one of those weeks.

## Segment C: Peer Check-In + Decision Log (1:50--2:45)

### 1:50--2:15 | Peer Check-In
**Format:** Pair work
**Language skills:** Speaking (describing work-in-progress, evaluating alignment), listening (partner's description), reading (partner's Goal Document and artifact)

"Find your partner. You're going to show each other what you've been building. But this isn't just 'look at my thing.' The question is: does this look like what you described in your Goal Document?"

Pairs take turns (~10 min each):
1. **Show:** Student shows their artifact-in-progress to their partner (3 min)
2. **Compare:** Partner opens the student's Goal Document (Weeks 7-8 entries, where the project was planned). Partner reads the plan, looks at the artifact. "Does this match? What's different?" (3 min)
3. **Feedback:** Partner says one thing that matches the plan and one thing that seems different or missing (2 min)

**Guidance on screen:** "When you look at your partner's work, ask yourself:
- Does this look like what they described in their Goal Document?
- What's here that they planned?
- What's missing?
- What's here that they didn't plan?"

Switch roles and repeat.

### 2:15--2:35 | Decision Log Entry
**Format:** Individual writing
**Language skills:** Writing (evaluative, technical)

"You've been building for an hour. You've heard your partner's feedback. Now document one significant AI interaction from today."

Students who are writing a Decision Log entry this week complete it now using the standard template (task goal, AI used, prompt snippets, output summary, decision, verification, what changed, self-assessment).

Students who are not writing a DL this week: continue project work or refine their process documentation notes.

### 2:35--2:40 | Commit to Portfolio
**Format:** Individual
**Language skills:** Writing (commit messages)

"Save what you've done today. Commit your work to your portfolio repository with a descriptive message."

Students commit their artifact-in-progress and any documentation to GitHub. Commit message should describe what was accomplished: "Added first draft of budget spreadsheet with charts" not "Updated files."

### 2:40--2:45 | Closing
**Format:** Whole class

- "You started building. Look at your task list from this morning. What did you finish? What didn't you get to? That's useful information for next week."
- "Next week: you finish and submit your mid-semester artifact package. That means the artifact itself, your process documentation, and evidence of sharing. Start thinking about what's complete and what still needs work."
- "Your artifact package includes three things: what you built, documentation of how you built it (in your own words), and evidence that you shared or explained it to someone. The peer check-in today counts as sharing."

## After Class

- **Instructor reviews task lists and process documentation notes** (spot-check, not all students). Look for: specificity of task lists, evidence of process documentation during building, whether students are on track to submit a complete artifact package next week.
- **Flag students who appear stuck or off-track** for a brief check-in at the start of Week 10.
- **Decision Log entries:** If students submitted entries this week, note them for the feedback round 2 batch (entries 3-5, returned by Week 10).

## Language Development Summary

- **Vocabulary:** 5 new terms (slide, layout, speaker notes, caption, colour contrast) pre-trained in Segment A and applied where relevant during project work
- **Speaking:** Brief pair sharing of task lists; sustained peer check-in (describing work-in-progress, evaluating alignment with stated goals, giving structured feedback); gallery walk discussion
- **Reading:** Partner's Goal Document (Weeks 7-8 entries); AI output during project work; other groups' gallery walk summaries; system feedback during building
- **Writing:** Task list as Goal Document entry (planning/imperative language); process documentation notes during building; Decision Log entry (evaluative); commit messages; gallery walk summary sentence
- **Listening:** Project-type demonstration; partner's description of their artifact-in-progress; partner's feedback on alignment

## Assessment Alignment

- **No graded submission this week** (artifact package due next week)
- **CLO 6** (Produce digital products): Students begin building their mid-semester artifact
- **CLO 3** (Guide AI tools): Task list writing; process documentation of AI interactions
- **CLO 4** (Evaluate AI text): Decision Log entry (optional this week); peer check-in evaluates whether AI-assisted building matches stated intentions
- **Decision Log entries:** Students choose whether to write an entry this week (counts toward 8 required entries across Weeks 2-13)
- **Goal Document:** The task list IS the Goal Document entry this week. Instructor does not read Goal Documents this week (next reading point is Week 13).
- **Process documentation:** Students should be accumulating notes for the artifact package (Week 10). Not collected yet.
